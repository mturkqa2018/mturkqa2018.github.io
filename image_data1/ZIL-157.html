The ZIL-157 is a general purpose ​2 ⁄2-ton 6x6 truck, produced post-World War II in the Soviet Union by ZiL. The ZIL-157 was the standard truck used by the army until it was replaced by the ZIL-131 and Ural-375 series that became the standard Soviet army trucks alongside the GAZ-66. The People's Liberation Army also operated the CA-30, a copy of the ZIL-157.

Variants[edit]

  • ZIL-157 (ЗИЛ-157) - cargo truck, produced 1958 - 1961
  • ZIL-157K (ЗИЛ-157К) - cargo truck, produced 1962 - 1978
  • ZIL-157KV (ЗИЛ-157КВ) - tractor unit, produced 1962 - 1978.
  • ZIL-157KD (ЗИЛ-157КД) - 5-ton cargo truck, produced since 1978
  • ZIL-157KDV (ЗИЛ-157КДВ) - tractor unit, produced since 1978
  • ZIL-165: Prototype for ZIL-131. Produced in 1958.
