Helix lucorum is a species of large, edible, air-breathing land snail, a terrestrial pulmonate gastropod mollusk in the family Helicidae, the typical snails.

Distribution[edit]

The native distribution is Eastern Mediterranean and Asia Minor.

  • Eastern Europe - East region of Black sea: Georgia and Ukraine
  • Southern Europe - Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Hungary, Italy, Macedonia, Romania, Sardinia, Yugoslavia, Turkey
  • Central Europe: Czech Republic (since 2009, introduced; as of 2011 the only locality is Prague-Å½iÅ¾kov) and Slovakia (since 2013, introduced; as of 2014 the only locality is in Bratislava)
  • Western Europe: France (introduced), Great Britain (since 2009, introduced, Wimbledon)
  • Asia: Israel
