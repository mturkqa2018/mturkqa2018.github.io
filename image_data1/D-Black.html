Desmond Kwesi Blackmore (born 12 January, 1987), known by his stage name D-Black, is a Ghanaian anglophone hip-hop star.

Career[edit]

D-Black is an entrepreneur, executive producer, and multiple award-winning Anglophone hip hop / afrobeats artist from Ghana. Born Desmond Kwesi Blackmore on the 12th of January 1987 to half Ghanaian, half English father, John Derek Blackmore and Ghanaian mother Adeline Boateng who hails from Koforidua in the Eastern Region. Outside of making music, D-Black is the C.E.O & director of the Black Avenue Group of Companies. With subsidiary firms, of which D-Black is director of namely “Black Avenue Muzik”, an independent Record Label and Distribution outlet with artists Wisa Greid, MTN Hitmaker winner Freda Rhymz , songstress Sefa , ‘Potato’ hitmaker Dahlin Gage as well as Canadain and Australian raised crooners Nina Ricchie, Osayo signed to the label.

D-Black owns “Black Avenue Clothing” an independent clothing line, “Livewire Events” co-owned by himself and his road manager and have produced festivals, concerts, boxing fights, soccer tournaments and more in Accra, Kumasi, Koforidua, Axim for the Ministry of Tourism, The Celebrity Soccer Weekend, BAM Festival, The BHIM FillTheDome Concert, Night Train Beach Jam, The Kundum Festival & more. He is also the CEO of “Black Avenue TV” and “Black Avenue Films”, two new television and film production companies with an award-winning movie and television show under their belt in their first year. D-Black also is the owner and CEO of Club Onyx, a plush nightclub located in the Cantonments neighborhood of Accra and Oasis Pool Lounge also in Cantonments – Accra. D-Black has recently opened up celebrity Advertising firm ‘Volcano’ with its offices located in North Kaneshie – Awudome Estates in Accra in the Black Avenue Group office complex. In 2014 D-Black was outdoored as the official brand ambassador for Ciroc Ultra Premium Vodka in Ghana and in 2016 the brand ambassador for Belaire LUC .

.

In 2019 through his Desmond Blackmore Enterprise D-Black is set to launch a Vodka brand and soap line via share acquisition and investments in already existing products.

With three solo albums under his belt in 2011 and 2013, 2016 “Music, Love & Life” , “The Revelation” & “Lightwork”, the rapper has received over 70 award nominations with 21 wins around the African continent including nods and wins at the Channel O Music Video Awards, The BET Awards, The Vodafone Ghana Music Awards, The MTN 4Syte Music Video Awards, The Sound City Awards, Ghana Music Awards UK and more. He recently signed a 2 year digital distribution deal with Universal Music, Africa for his next album and has performed in over 40 cities including London, Coventry, Manchester (U.K) New York, Boston, D.C (USA), Ottawa, Montreal, Edmonton (Canada), Italy, Holland, Johannesburg, Capetown (South Africa) and more. D-Black has performed / opened for international acts including Chris Brown, Rick Ross, Jay Z, Busta Rhymes, Akon, Sean Paul and more on various stages.

The young entertainer, in his 9 year career has churned out chart topping hits including "Vera", "Change Your Life ft. EL", "My Kinda Girl ft. Sarkodie", "Personal Person and Seihor" with Castro, "Get on da dancefloor", "Somebody" ft. Kwabena Kwabena, "Red Card", "Kotomoshi", "Badder" ft. Kuami Eugene and more.

He has in the past few years, collaborated with other African A- List acts including Nigeria’s Davido, Waje, Phyno, Seyi Shay, Sean Tizzle, Mo Cheddah, South Africa’s TearGas, Cassper Nyovest, Donald, Kenya’s STL, Zimbabwe’s Buffalo Souljah, Stanley Enow from Cameroon, Fally Ipupa from Congo, Vanessa Mdee from Tanzania and some of Ghana’s most talented acts including Sarkodie, Castro, Stonebwoy, E.L, Efya, Kuami Eugene, Kidi, King Promise, Shatta Wale and more.

In 2010 D-Black was nominated at the BET Awards in the USA for Best African Act following the release of his first studio album "Music, Love & Life" that spawned the award-winning hits "Somebody FT. Kwabena Kwabena" and "Get On Da Dancefloor ft. Dr. Cryme". His first nationwide tour 'The Yes Boss Tour' as a massive success as he sold out stadia and auditoriums in 10 cities in Ghana.

D-Black was featured on the 2011 BET (BLACK ENTERTAINMENT TELEVISION) HIPHOP AWARDS CYPHER with 6 other Ghanaian rappers; Reggie Rockstone, Sarkodie, Edem, Tinny, Kwaku-t & Baby G. This was the first time in the history of the BETHIPHOP AWARDS, making them the first West African Artists to be featured on the BET CYPHER. This historical feat was directed by BET’S Lilian Blankson and aired on the 16TH of October 2010 on the BET INTERNATIONAL NETWORK all over the world to audiences in the U.K, Ireland, Middle East, Australia and Africa. Other artists that have been featured on the BET CYPHER include Kanye West, Nicki Minaj, Eminem, Fabolous, Ace Hood, Juelz Santana, Common, KRS 1, Ice Cube, Jadakiss, K’Naan and more.

D-Black released three singles off his 2012 'The Revelation' album "My Kinda Girl" featuring fellow Ghanaian rapper Sarkodie, "Falling" featuring Nigeria's Mo Cheddah and the smash hit single "Vera" made big rounds on Ghanaian and African radio and television. The azonto club banger "Vera" featuring Joey B was the number 1 song on the Ghanaian urban music charts on over 15 Ghanaian radio stations 12 weeks after its release and was the most popular song in Ghana through 2012.

D-Black also won awards for "Song of the Year" and "Collaboration of the Year" for his hit track "Vera" at the 2013 Ghana Music Awards, U.K in August 2013. D-Black has performed with Jay Z, Chris Brown, Fat Joe, Akon, Busta Rhymes , Stevie Wonder, Omarion, Lyold at concerts in Ghana. D-black has performed in countries including the USA, (UAE) Dubai, Italy, Holland, South Africa, U.K, Nigeria and more. In July 2013 he headlined at the O2 Arena at the U. K. Afrobeats Music Festival Concert with label mate Joey B, which kicked off his U.S.A Tour in September 2013 and the first leg of a European tour in February 2014 in Amsterdam, Holland, Bologna, Italy, London, United Kingdom, Coventry.

In 2014, D-Black begun working in the studio on his third album 'Lightwork'. The first single off this upcoming album, Personal Person featuring Ghanaian crooner Castro, was released in April 2014 and received massive airplay on Ghanaian and African radio.

In July 2015, Personal Person was the #1 song on the top 10 charts across Ghana. Castro doubled up with his 'Seihor' record featuring D-Black that also shot to number 1 on all radio and TV charts in Ghana. The second single off the album 'Kotomoshi' also spawned another monster hit record. D-Black took a break from his musical journey soon after his friend and collaborator, Castro’s demise for close to two years after this and established 4 new business, a night club, a lounge, advertising firm and re launched the Black Avenue Muzik label.

He released his Hunger and Thirst mixtape online in 2017 and is currently in the studio wrapping up his fourth studio album 'Loyalty' set to be released in April 2019.
