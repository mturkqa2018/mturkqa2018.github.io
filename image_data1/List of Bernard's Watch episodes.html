Original series[edit]

Series 1 (1997)[edit]

No. Episode Writer/s Director/s Original Airdate
1"Still Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham14 November 1997 (1997-11-14)
Bernard receives a watch from the postman because he is always late for everything including his friend Karen's birthday party.
2"A Bit More Time"TBADavid Cobham21 November 1997 (1997-11-21)
As Bernard gets used to using his magic watch, he finds it comes in very handy at school.
3"Time Out"TBADavid Cobham28 November 1997 (1997-11-28)
Bernard wants to go to the zoo, but his parents just don't have the time. Bernard, however, has all the time in the world, and decides to clear his parents' schedules a bit.
4"Time For Everything"TBADavid Cobham5 December 1997 (1997-12-05)
While practising his magic tricks, Bernard comes across a lost rabbit outside his house and decides to take care of it. When he finds who the rabbit actually belongs to, Bernard performs a special trick to reunite owner and pet in style.
5"Giving Time"TBADavid Cobham12 December 1997 (1997-12-12)
Bernard stays at his grandad while his parents are away, Bernard is amazed at how much time his grandad has! Bernard's parents come back as his grandad slips and Bernard saves him.

Series 2 (1998)[edit]

No. Episode Writer/s Director/s Original Airdate
1"Plenty of Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham2 October 1998 (1998-10-02)
Karen discovers Bernard's watch, and finds out what it can do.
2"Story Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham9 October 1998 (1998-10-09)
Karen finds it very difficult to write stories at school, so she uses the watch to give herself more time to think. But then she finds herself trapped in time when she loses the watch.
3"Quality Time"TBADavid Cobham16 October 1998 (1998-10-16)
Karen has often fancied the idea of having a baby sibling. But then she has to babysit her neighbour's naughty toddler, and discovers just how much time young children can take up.
4"Games Time"TBADavid Cobham23 October 1998 (1998-10-23)
Bernard wants to go and see his friend Karen playing in the Tennis tournament but at the same time he has to play in the Football final. What is he going to do?
5"Time Travel"TBADavid Cobham30 October 1998 (1998-10-30)
Bernard is accidentally left behind as his family return from their holiday. How will he catch up with his parents again?
6"Show Time"TBADavid Cobham6 November 1998 (1998-11-06)
On Bernard's birthday, Grandad performs a conjuring show for his party. However, the show looks like coming a cropper thanks to a rude party guest. Then Bernard performs a few tricks of his own, and soon the tables are turned.
7"Time Tables"TBADavid Cobham13 November 1998 (1998-11-13)
Bernard gets into trouble with his music teacher when Mr Beasley accidentally drives off with Bernard's instrument. At first, Bernard is inclined to seek revenge on his teacher, but ends up learning a lot about the true value of time.
8"Gnome Time"Brian FinchDavid Cobham20 November 1998 (1998-11-20)
While on his own in the house one day, Bernard finds himself being burgled. Naturally, the magic watch proves very handy, but Bernard also has some unlikely allies on his side.
9"Time to Spare"TBADavid Cobham27 November 1998 (1998-11-27)
Due to a fast clock, Bernard arrives at the go-kart track a whole hour early for a friend's birthday party. His friend Dylan is also early, so they hang out in the park together, but then Dylan gets his head stuck in some railings. Bernard goes in search of help...and one thing leads to another.
10"Swim Time"TBADavid Cobham6 December 1998 (1998-12-06)
Karen is in trouble at the swimming pool when she loses the key to her locker trapping the watch inside. She gets into more trouble when she finds a locker thief, with a master key, who tries to frame her.
11"Old Time"TBADavid Cobham11 December 1998 (1998-12-11)
A nasty museum employee gets Bernard's Grandad sacked from his job. Can Bernard put things right?
12"Space Time"TBADavid Cobham18 December 1998 (1998-12-18)
Karen makes a strange new friend one day in town, and uses the watch when her friend is in danger of missing her ship.

No. Episode Writer/s Director/s Original Airdate

7"Four Four Time"Richard FegenDavid Cobham5 November 1999 (1999-11-05)
Karen's mother's car breaks down when Karen is going to her music exam.
8"Tee Time"TBADavid Cobham12 November 1999 (1999-11-12)

9"Visiting Time"TBADavid Cobham19 November 1999 (1999-11-19)
Bernard's demanding cousin Lucy is staying with him, and looking after her takes all the time the watch can give. However, Lucy soon changes her ways when Bernard does a particularly good deed for her.
10"Borrowed Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham26 November 1999 (1999-11-26)
Karen is sick of her classmate Fergus always borrowing her things without asking. No one understands, so Karen uses the watch to give Fergus just the lesson he deserves.
11"Time, Gentleman"Richard FegenDavid Cobham6 December 1999 (1999-12-06)
This episode is a pilot episode, and was the first run of Bernard's watch, it is very rare, and only ever shown once on the children's channel citv, Bernard is trying to raise money for a local Pet Sanctuary, and his Grandad agrees to help by giving a darts masterclass. Unfortunately he isn't as good as he once claimed to be, Bernard is forced to intervene.
12"Another Time"TBADavid Cobham10 December 1999 (1999-12-10)
Karen loves looking in other people's houses in frozen time, She especially loved looking in Mrs Sadge's house as it was very old and because she is old Karen decided to help her out and Mrs Sadge thinks Karen is an elf.
13"Ending Time"TBADavid Cobham17 December 1999 (1999-12-17)
Bernard wakes up on New Year's Eve to find that his watch has been stolen by an arch-criminal Mr Rattle. Mr Rattle is mean to Bernard and his mother knocks Mr Rattle out with a vase. When he finally gets it back, the villain's deeds have made the watch stop working. Could the new millennium be the end of Bernard's watch?

11"Time to Heal"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham1 December 2000 (2000-12-01)

12"Play Time"TBADavid Cobham8 December 2000 (2000-12-08)
Lucy is acting in the school play, but shortly before opening night, the leading male injures himself. The play may have to be cancelled, as no one can understudy in such a short time. That is, unless they happen to have a magic watch...
13"Time's Up"TBADavid Cobham15 December 2000 (2000-12-15)
Bernard's family are off to America on holiday, when suddenly the Postman arrives to tell Bernard that he must return the watch. But why?

Series 5 (2001)[edit]

No. Episode Writer Director/s Original Airdate
1"Changing Times"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham26 November 2001 (2001-11-26)
Bernard can't wait to get his watch back when he arrives back from America, but finds it needs to be repaired, and in the meantime is given an alarm clock by the Postman. Everyone is wondering why Bernard came back home.
2"A Very Important Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham27 November 2001 (2001-11-27)
After receiving an unexpected visit from the Postman, Sam accidentally messes up her future. Can she correct her mistake to put the course of time right again?
3"Police Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham28 November 2001 (2001-11-28)
Bernard's Grandad has been arrested for a series of strange crimes. Bernard uses his watch to find out what has happened, and everyone is amazed to discover who the real culprit is.
4"Every Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham29 November 2001 (2001-11-29)
Bernard uses his watch to help people with their injuries. But when his teacher Mrs. Spindell sees him with his watch, she tells him not to bring it to school because it is valuable, but when he brings it anyway, she sends him to the headteacher.
5"Alien Times"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham30 November 2001 (2001-11-30)
A group of unlikely detectives spy on Sam as she uses the watch, and think she may be some form of mutant.
6"Doing Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham and Alison Rashley3 December 2000 (2000-12-03)
The Postman is distraught when his van runs away through time, as it may lead to him losing his job. Bernard soon helps to retrieve it, however - but in the process, he has a terrifying vision of the future. Is his destiny as dark as it appears?
7"Running on Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham4 December 2001 (2001-12-04)
Bernard receives a model train, but has no tracks to see it run on. He makes a friend in someone with a large model railway, Mr. Arnold. But when Mr. Arnold finds a note from his wife saying she's left him, can his knowledge of trains and Bernard's watch help save their marriage?
8"A Worrying Time"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham5 December 2001 (2001-12-05)
Sam tries to cure her aunt Rowena of worrying all the time by trying to get her to try high-risk activities, but she is too worried. But when Sam ends up in danger, can her aunt get over her fears to save Sam?
9"Pool Time"Andrew NorrissAlison Rashley6 December 2001 (2001-12-06)
Bernard and Sam like to use a local hotel swimming pool. But one day, the hotel is in danger of being closed due to lost mice. Can the magic watch help?
10"What's Time For?"Andrew NorrissDavid Cobham7 December 2001 (2001-12-07)
The Postman needs Bernard's help to handle a mischievous young watch-holder, Harry Claymore, who keeps causing problems for everyone.
