This is a list of earthquakes in 1943. Only magnitude 6.0 or greater earthquakes appear on the list. Lower magnitude events are included if they have caused death, injury, or damage. Events which occurred in remote areas will be excluded from the list as they wouldn't have generated significant media interest. All dates are listed according to UTC time. The planet experienced an unusually busy year, with twenty-nine earthquakes that reached 7.0 or greater. The largest occurred in Chile and measured a magnitude of 8.1, but Indonesia, the Philippines, Turkey, the United States, Japan and Russia all had large events above magnitude 7.0. The year was characterised by a number of quakes that caused substantial deaths. Faring worst was Turkey, which had three separate deadly events, including the deadliest of the year in November. Japan had an earthquake in September which caused 1,400 deaths.

Notable events[edit]

January[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.3 earthquake struck Surxondaryo Region, Uzbekistan on January 11 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • An earthquake struck Republika Srpska, Bosnia and Herzegovina on January 29. The magnitude and depth were unknown. Nineteen deaths and some damage were caused.
  • A magnitude 6.1 earthquake struck Guayas Province, Ecuador on January 30 at a depth of 58.7 km. Many homes sustained damage.
  • A magnitude 5.0 earthquake struck Cusco Region, Peru on January 31 at an unknown depth. Seventy-five people were killed and 200 injured. Some damage was reported. This was a high number of deaths and injuries for such a moderate magnitude.

February[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck off the coast of Pakistan on February 6 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Puno Region, Peru on February 16 at a depth of 120.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck off the coast of Guerrero, Mexico on February 22 at a depth of 20.0 km. Eleven people were killed and major damage was caused.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Badakhshan Province, Afghanistan on February 28 at a depth of 207.5 km.

March[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck the South Sandwich Islands on March 9 at a depth of 20.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck southeast of the Loyalty Islands, New Caledonia on March 14 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck Tarapaca Region, Chile on March 14 at a depth of 120.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.9 earthquake struck southeast of the Loyalty Islands, New Caledonia on March 15 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck the western chain of the Caroline Islands on March 15 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Fiji on March 15 at a depth of 297.9 km.
  • A magnitude 6.6 earthquake struck West New Britain Province, New Britain, Papua New Guinea on March 21 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.9 earthquake struck Fiji on March 26 at a depth of 85.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.0 earthquake struck just off the west coast of Madagascar on March 29 at a depth of 35.0 km.

April[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.1 earthquake struck the Sunda Strait, Indonesia on April 1 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Region, Tajikistan on April 5 at a depth of 100.0 km. Many (at least 101) deaths were reported. Many homes were wrecked.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck San Martin Region, Peru on April 5 at a depth of 140.0 km.
  • A magnitude 8.1 earthquake struck Coquimbo Region, Chile on April 6 at a depth of 35.0 km. Eleven people died and some damage was caused. A small tsunami was reported.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck the Northern Mariana Islands on April 9 at a depth of 157.5 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck off the east coast of Honshu, Japan on April 11 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 5.0 earthquake struck Bordj Bou Arreridj Province, Algeria on April 16. The depth was unknown. Some (at least 51) deaths were caused.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck south of Fiji on April 28 at a depth of 530.0 km.

May[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck south of Panama on May 2 at a depth of 20.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck southeast of Luzon, Philippines on May 3 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck Coquimbo Region, Chile on May 22 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck east of Mindanao, Philippines on May 25 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck off the west coast of Mexico on May 26 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck Fiji on May 28 at a depth of 630.0 km.

June[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck Tonga on June 3 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck southern Sumatra, Indonesia on June 8 at a depth of 15.0 km. This was followed by a larger quake the next morning. This can be considered a doublet earthquake.
  • A magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck southern Sumatra, Indonesia on June 9 at a depth of 10.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck off the northeast coast of Honshu, Japan on June 13 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 aftershock struck off the northeast coast of Honshu, Japan on June 13 at a depth of 0.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.4 aftershock struck off the northeast coast of Honshu, Japan on June 13 at a depth of 31.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck Kocaeli Province, Turkey on June 20 at a depth of 35.0 km. Major damage and 285 deaths were caused in the area.
  • A magnitude 5.0 earthquake struck under Chengdu, Sichuan Province, China on June 21 at an unknown depth. At least one person died and a few homes collapsed.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck the Celebes Sea, Indonesia on June 29 at a depth of 180.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck the Flores Sea, Indonesia on June 30 at a depth of 720.0 km.

July[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Arequipa Region, Peru on July 5 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.7 earthquake struck south of the Kermadec Islands, New Zealand on July 11 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck off the south coast of Java, Indonesia on July 23 at a depth of 60.0 km. Two-hundred and thirteen people were killed, 2,096 were injured, and 2,800 homes were damaged.
  • A magnitude 7.7 earthquake struck north of Puerto Rico on July 29 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 aftershock struck northwest of Puerto Rico on July 30 at a depth of 35.0 km.

August[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Vanuatu on August 1 at a depth of 225.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.7 earthquake struck Southland District, South Island, New Zealand on August 2 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Escuintla Department, Guatemala on August 31 at a depth of 87.5 km.

September[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck north of Macquarie Island on September 6 at a depth of 10.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck Badakhshan Province, Afghanistan on September 9 at a depth of 200.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Tottori Prefecture, Honshu, Japan on September 10 at a depth of 15.0 km. Fourteen-hundred people died and 7,739 homes were destroyed.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck southeast of the Loyalty Islands on September 14 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.7 earthquake struck southeast of the Loyalty Islands on September 14 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.9 earthquake struck the Kermadec Islands, New Zealand on September 14 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck north of North Island, New Zealand on September 22 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Quetzaltenango Department, Guatemala on September 23 at a depth of 91.8 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan on September 24 at a depth of 120.0 km.

October[edit]

  • A magnitude 6.0 earthquake struck Nagano Prefecture, Japan on October 13 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck the Dodecanese Islands, Greece on October 16 at a depth of 110.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck Assam, India on October 23 at a depth of 15.0 km. Some damage was reported in Burma.

November[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.6 earthquake struck southern Alaska on November 3 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.7 earthquake struck the Aru Islands, Indonesia on November 6 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck the Kuril Islands, Russia on November 9 at a depth of 65.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck Vanuatu on November 13 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck off the south coast of Honshu, Japan on November 17 at a depth of 335.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck off the east coast of Taiwan on November 24 at a depth of 20.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.1 earthquake struck southern Sumatra, Indonesia on November 26 at a depth of 140.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.5 earthquake struck Cankiri Province, Turkey on November 26 at a depth of 20.0 km. Four-thousand and twenty people died with another 5,000 injured, and 40,000 homes were destroyed.
  • A magnitude 7.6 earthquake struck western Kamchatka Krai, Russia on November 28 at a depth of 350.0 km. Some damage was caused.
  • A magnitude 6.4 earthquake struck Catamarca Province, Argentina on November 29 at a depth of 15.0 km.

December[edit]

  • A magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck East Sepik Province, Papua New Guinea on December 1 at a depth of 119.7 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck Tarapaca Region, Chile on December 1 at a depth of 114.2 km.
  • A magnitude 6.8 earthquake struck off the east coast of Taiwan on December 2 at a depth of 15.0 km.
  • An earthquake struck Erzincan Province, Turkey on December 5. The magnitude and depth were unknown. Major damage occurred and 550 people were killed.
  • A magnitude 6.0 earthquake struck near the border of Oaxaca and Chiapas, Mexico on December 7 at a depth of 60.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck Pastaza Province, Ecuador on December 22 at a depth of 130.0 km.
  • A magnitude 6.5 earthquake struck off the northeast coast of Colombia on December 22 at a depth of 35.0 km.
  • A magnitude 7.0 earthquake struck west of Bougainville Island, Papua New Guinea on December 23 at a depth of 35.0 km.
