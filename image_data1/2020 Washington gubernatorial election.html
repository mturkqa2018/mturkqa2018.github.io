The 2020 Washington gubernatorial election will be held on November 3, 2020, to elect the Governor of Washington, concurrently with the 2020 U.S. presidential election, as well as elections to the United States Senate and elections to the United States House of Representatives and various state and local elections. As Washington does not have gubernatorial term limits, incumbent Democratic Governor Jay Inslee is eligible to run for re-election to a third term in office, but has announced a bid for the presidency in 2020.

Candidates[edit]

Democratic Party[edit]

Potential[edit]

  • Lisa Brown, Director of the Washington Department of Commerce
  • Dow Constantine, King County Executive
  • Bob Ferguson, Washington Attorney General
  • Hilary Franz, Washington Commissioner of Public Lands
  • Jay Inslee, incumbent Governor of Washington

Declined[edit]

  • Mike Pellicciotti, state representative

Republican Party[edit]

Potential[edit]

  • Bill Bryant, former Seattle Port Commissioner and nominee for governor in 2016
  • Doug Ericksen, State Senator (42nd District)
  • Drew C. MacEwen, State Representative (35th District)
  • Dave Reichert, former U.S. Congressman
  • Drew Stokesbary, State Representative (31st District) and House Republican budget writer
  • J.T. Wilcox, State Representative (2nd District) and House Minority Leader

Declined[edit]

  • Bruce Dammeier, Pierce County Executive
