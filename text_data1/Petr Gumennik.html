Personal life

Gumennik was born on 11 April 2002 in Saint Petersburg, Russia. 3

Career

Early years

Gumennik began learning to skate in 2006. In March 2014, he won gold at the Russian Youth Championships â€“ Younger Age. The following season, he placed fifth at the Russian Junior Championships and repeated as the champion at the Russian Youth Championships â€“ Younger Age. 4

2015â€“2016 season

In August 2015, Gumennik debuted on the ISU Junior Grand Prix (JGP) series, coached by Oleg Tataurov, Tatiana Mishina, and Alexei Mishin. He placed sixth at the 2015 JGP in Riga, Latvia, and then fourth in ToruÅ„, Poland. At the 2016 Russian Junior Championships, he finished sixth. 5

2016â€“2017 season

In September, Gumennik competed at two 2016 JGP events, placing fourth in Saransk, Russia, and then fifth in Tallinn, Estonia. He placed ninth at the 2017 Russian Junior Championships. During the season, he was coached by Alexei Mishin. 6

2017â€“2018 season

Veronika Daineko and Vladislav Sesganov became his coaches in 2017. Gumennik missed most of the 2017â€“2018 season but finished eighth at the 2018 Russian Junior Championships and won silver at the Russian Youth Championships â€“ Elder Age. 7

2018â€“2019 season

Coached by Daineko, Gumennik started his season with a gold medal at the 2018 JGP event in Vancouver, Canada. He ranked fourth in the short program but won the free skate and outscored the silver medalist, Tomoki Hiwatashi, by a margin of about seven points. At this event he scored his personal best score of 220.04 points and his free skating score of 150.35 points was the junior men's new record score. 8

At his second JGP event of the season he won another gold medal, now in Ljubljana, Slovenia. He was ranked first in both the short program and the free skate, and again he beat the same silver medalist, Tomoki Hiwatashi, this time by about 4 points. With two JGP gold medals Gumennik qualified for the 2018â€“19 Junior Grand Prix Final, where he won the silver medal after placing third in the short program and second in the free skate. In January, he took gold at the 2019 Skate Helena. 9

In February 2019, Gumennik won the silver medal at the 2019 Russian Junior Championships after placing first in the short program and fourth in the free skate. Assigned to the 2019 World Junior Championships, he was fourth in the short program with a new personal best. 10
