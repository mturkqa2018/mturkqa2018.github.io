PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a lies at the end of a deep natural bay on the northern coast of BraÄ, which in the summer months gives its harbour protection from the winds of the open sea. Archeological findings, such as an altar of Jupiter and a Roman grave stele, show that Stijepanska luka was settled in Roman times. Only after 1420 with the establishment of the Venetian rule, when times became calmer again, the people moved again from the inland to the coastal area. Stonemasons and artists working with the local stone made the material famous during the Renaissance. Examples of such artists were Giorgio da Sebenico (1410-1473) or Andreas Alessi (1425-1505). PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a became one of the main cultural centers of BraÄ. 1516 the first private school of the island was founded. PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a's history followed the history of Dalmatia: the Venetian rule ended in 1797 after almost four centuries, and in the next two decades, BraÄ repeatedly changed hands between France, Italy, and Austria-Hungary, and was even conquered by Montenegrin forces with Russian support for a short time. During World War II, in 1943, an attack of the Italian army damaged the city heavily. 15

Economy

Stone has traditionally been one of the main sources of income of PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a. The stonemason school, founded in 1906, builds on centuries of tradition. It is the only such school in Croatia. 22

The main lime stone quarries are at the coast, east of the bay of PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a. The largest one is Veselje ('Happiness'), which belongs to Jadrankamen, the largest European quarry company. Jadrankamen was founded in PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a in 1902, and is still headquartered here. The shining white of Veselje is clearly visible from the main land and from aerial pictures. Veselje is already mentioned in documents from 1455, when it was used by Georgio da Sebenico (Croatian Juraj Dalmatinac). A story that is often heard locally is that some of the pure white limestone for the White House came from the quarries near PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a. Other quarries close to PuÄiÅ¡Ä‡a are TesiÅ¡Ä‡e, Punta, and Kupinovo. A number of historic quarries, going back to Roman times, are located close to the town. 23

Besides stone, the main sources of income have traditionally been winemaking, fishing, olives, husbandry (mainly sheep and goats), and, more recently, tourism. 24
