Ken Skupski Jr. (born 9 April 1983) is a British tennis player, best known as a doubles player, who has regularly partnered with his younger brother Neal Skupski since 2013. 2

His breakthrough year was 2009, when he won his first two ATP tournaments and broke into the world's top 50 doubles players. 3

At the 2010 Commonwealth Games, Skupski and Ross Hutchins won the Doubles Silver Medal. In the Mixed Doubles, Skupski and Sarah Borwell beat Ross Hutchins and Anna Smith to win the Bronze Medal. 4

His best result at a grand slam came at Wimbledon 2017, reaching the quarter-finals of both the men's doubles and mixed doubles. 5

2013

Although initially partnering with Delgado, Skupski increasingly played during 2013 with his brother Neal.
