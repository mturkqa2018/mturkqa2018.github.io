History

The Qi court commissioned Shen Yue in 487 to complete the Book of Song. 3

Contents

The first ten juan are annals of the Liu Song emperors, 30 juan contain treatises, and 60 juan are dedicated to biographies. 4

Translations

There are no known full translations to English. Dien includes partial translations of The Disputation at Pengcheng, a conflict in present-day Xuzhou. Dien compares the Northern Wei and Liu Song accounts of this one in a long series of conflicts between the two states. The Liu Song account is included in volume 59 of the Book of Song. Holcombe translates a fragment of volume 54 on a land petition. 5
