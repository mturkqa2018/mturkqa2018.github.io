The 10Â cm Feldhaubitze M 99 was a howitzer used by Austria-Hungary during World War I. 1

It had a bronze barrel and lacked a modern recoil system, using only an ineffective spring-mounted spade brake, and was virtually obsolescent on its introduction. Nonetheless, it was the standard field howitzer for the Austrian Army at the outbreak of the war. 2

It donated its barrel to the 10 cm Gebirgshaubitze M 99, although few of those were made, being largely replaced by a version of the standard Feldhaubitze M 99 with a narrow, 1.3 metres (51Â in) carriage for use on mountain paths. The axle seats were deleted from these narrow carriages in the interest of saving weight. 3
