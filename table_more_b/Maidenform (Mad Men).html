"Maidenform" is the sixth episode in the second season of the American television drama series Mad Men. It was written by Matthew Weiner and directed by Phil Abraham. The episode originally aired on the AMC channel in the United States on August 31, 2008. 1

The focus returns to Don in his office and Roger Sterling (John Slattery) enters to tell Don to meet with Duck for lunch and put their argument about the American Airlines account to rest. The scene changes again to Duck's office where his children inform him that his ex-wife is remarrying and leaving the family dog with Duck. 5

As Duck leaves his office the camera returns to Don's office as Peggy, Salvatore, Ken Cosgrove (Aaron Staton), Paul Kinsey (Michael Gladis), and Frederick Rumsen (Joel Murray) enter to discuss the Playtex campaign. Peggy finds out that the others had had an informal, after-hours, discussion about the campaign and Kinsey, who had not originally been assigned to it, pushes his way onto the account with his idea. Kinsey reveals his plan to compare women to Jackie Kennedy and Marilyn Monroe for the Playtex campaign, and, as they leave the office, Peggy tells Freddy she wants to be included in any future after-hours work. 6

Don stops by Duck's office rather than meeting him for lunch where he accuses Duck of pitching the client's idea to Don more than pitching Don's ideas to the clients. Duck insists that attempting to gain American Airlines business was worthwhile as it made people reconsider what they thought about Sterling-Cooper before asking Don if they can move on. Don agrees and leaves. 7

There is a brief scene where Bobbie reveals that she also has a daughter that Don didn't know about before the focus changes to Peggy who had just found out that the men on the account are holding a casting call for a Playtex model without telling her. 8

The following morning Betty is shown wearing a bikini purchased from the club fashion show and Don tells her she looks desperate. 9

They apologize for the â€œgoose-chaseâ€ and leave, not before inviting the team for a night on the town on them. 11

There is a brief scene with Duck who considers drinking a bottle of liquor before abandoning his dog on the sidewalk outside the office. This is followed by a scene with Don and Bobbie where Bobbie reveals that women have been talking about Don's many affairs and ability in the bedroom. This knowledge enrages Don who ties Bobbie to the bed and leaves. 12

The scene changes again and reveals the Playtex executives at a strip club with the creative team from Sterling-Cooper, minus Peggy Olsen who shows up a few minutes later dressed in a dress more revealing that any she has yet been seen in. 13

In the last scene of the episode Don is seen shaving, watched by his daughter Sally (Kiernan Shipka). Something Don sees in the mirror bothers him and sends Sally away before staring blankly at nothing as the camera zooms out. 14
