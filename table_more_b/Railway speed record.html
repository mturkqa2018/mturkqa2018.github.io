Determination of the fastest rail vehicle in the world varies depending on the definition of "rail". 1

The world record for conventional wheeled passenger trains is held by France's specially tuned TGV (Train Ã  Grande Vitesse). Reduced to three cars, it broke the world record in April 2007, reaching 574.8Â km/h (357.2Â mph) on a 140Â km section of track. 2

The maximum speed attained by a passenger train is held by Japan's experimental maglev train L0 Series, having achieved 603Â km/h (375Â mph) on a 42.8Â km magnetic levitation track in April 2015. 3

The fastest manned rail vehicle was a manned rocket sled, which carried United States Air Force flight surgeon Colonel John Stapp at 1,017Â km/h (632Â mph). 4

Unmanned rocket sleds that ride on rails have reached over 10,400Â km/h (6,460Â mph), equivalent to Mach 8.5. 5
