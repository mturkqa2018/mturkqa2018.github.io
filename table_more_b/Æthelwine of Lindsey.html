Ã†thelwine (died c. 700) was the second bishop of Lindsey from around 680, and is regarded as a saint. 1

Other than a couple of references in Bede's Historia to Ã†thelwine and his family, very little is known of him. One brother, named Edilhun (i.e. Ã†thelhun), a "youth of great capacity of the English nobility", is said by Bede to have died of the plague while visiting a monastery in Ireland in the year 664. Another brother, Aldwin, was abbot at Partney, and a sister, Ã†thelhild, was an abbess. Bede tells of her visiting Queen Osthryth at Bardney Abbey in about 697. She was still alive when Bede was writing in the 720s. 2

Ã†thelwine probably died around 700. His feast day is 3 May or 29 June. The even less well evidenced Saint Aldwyn is sometimes identified with his brother. 3
