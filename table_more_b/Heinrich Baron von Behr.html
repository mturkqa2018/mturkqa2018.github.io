Heinrich von Behr (26 JuneÂ [O.S. 13 June]Â 1902 â€“ 14 August 1983) was a German general during World War II. He was a recipient of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves of Nazi Germany. Behr surrendered to the British troops in April 1945 and was held until 1947. He later served in the Bundeswehr of West Germany, retiring in 1962 at the rank of a general. 1

Awards and decorations

  • Iron Cross (1939) 2nd Class (20 April 1940) & 1st Class (24 June 1940)
  • Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves
    • Knight's Cross on 23 February 1944 as Oberst and commander of Panzer-Grenadier-Regiment 200
    • 689th Oak Leaves on 9 January 1945 as Oberst and commander of Panzer-Grenadier-Regiment 200
  • Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany (19 September 1962)
