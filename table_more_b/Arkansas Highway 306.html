Route description

Cotton Plant to Becton

The route begins in Cotton Plant near the eastern terminus of HighwayÂ 38 near a junction with HighwayÂ 17. HighwayÂ 306 runs north through cotton country and the community of Shady Grove until meeting WoodruffÂ CRÂ 762 and CRÂ 780, where state maintenance ends. In an extremely rare circumstance, the highway designation continues along CRÂ 780 in name only as an officially designated exception of 2.24 miles (3.60Â km). This overlap ceases at a junction with HighwayÂ 17 at which HighwayÂ 306 begins again and runs for 0.05 miles (0.080Â km) before state maintenance ends again. The roadway continues as CRÂ 680. 3

Hunter to AR 284

HighwayÂ 306 begins at the northern town limits of Hunter at USÂ 49. The highway runs east to Holliday before angling northeast and entering St. Francis County. HighwayÂ 306 continues northeast, serving as the southern terminus of HighwayÂ 259 and passing through the communities of Macedonia and Pine Tree, after which the route begins to curve east. Following an intersection with the southern terminus of HighwayÂ 193, the highway intersects HighwayÂ 1 in Colt and passes the William Stone House, a property on the National Register of Historic Places. The highway exits Colt heading east and winds through forested areas. The route terminates at HighwayÂ 284 near Village Creek State Park and Summer Sweet Lake. 4

HighwayÂ 306 begins at CrossÂ CRÂ 825, which runs parallel to the St. Francis River levee. The highway runs due east through center pivot irrigation cropland to terminate at HighwayÂ 75 at Gieseck, south of Parkin. 5

History

The road from Hunter east to the St. Francis county line was designated a state highway on April 24, 1963. The HighwayÂ 306 segments running north from Cotton Plant and west from Gieseck were added to the state highway system on November 23, 1966. 6
