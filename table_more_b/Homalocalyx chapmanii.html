Homalocalyx chapmanii is a member of the Myrtaceae family endemic to Western Australia. 2

The shrub typically grows to a height of 0.2 to 0.5 metres (0.7 to 1.6Â ft). It blooms between September and October producing red-pink-purple flowers. 3

It is found on undulating plains and among weather granite in the northern Wheatbelt region of Western Australia between Carnamah and Three Springs where it grows in sandy soils. 4
