Ploudalmézeau (Breton: Gwitalmeze) is a commune in the Finistère department of Brittany in north-western France. 1

The village and small port of Portsall is part of the commune. Portsall is known for the Amoco Cadiz oil spill. 2

Amoco Cadiz oil spill

1

Amoco Cadiz anchor in Portsall

On 16 March 1978, Amoco Cadiz, a very large crude carrier (VLCC), owned by Amoco, split in two after running aground on Portsall Rocks, 5 km (3.1 mi) from the coast of Portsall, resulting in the largest oil spill of its kind in history to that date. 6
