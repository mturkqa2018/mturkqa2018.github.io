State RouteÂ 32 (SRÂ 32) is a 188.8-mile-long (303.8Â km) state highway that travels west-to-east through portions of Terrell, Lee, Worth, Turner, Irwin, Coffee, Bacon, Pierce, Brantley, and Glynn counties in the southern part of the U.S. state of Georgia. The highway connects Dawson with Sterling, via Leesburg, Ashburn, Fitzgerald, Douglas, and Alma. 2

History

1920s

SRÂ 32 was established at leas as early as 1919 from SRÂ 1 in Cuthbert east-southeast to Dawson, southeast to Albany, east-southeast to Sylvester, northeast to Ashburn, east-southeast to the approximate location of Nicholls, and east-northeast to SRÂ 15 in Alma. By the end of September 1921, the western terminus was truncated to SRÂ 7 in Sycamore. The former path of SRÂ 32 from Cuthbert to Sylvester was redesignated as SRÂ 50. SRÂ 32 was established on a segment from SRÂ 27 in Baxley east-northeast and north-northwest to SRÂ 23 in the southeastern part of Reidsville, with the Almaâ€“Baxley segment in-between proposed. By October 1926, USÂ 41 was designated on SRÂ 7 in Sycamore. USÂ 1 was designated on SRÂ 32 on the then-completed Almaâ€“Baxley segment. By October 1929, the eastern terminus of SRÂ 32 was truncated to USÂ 1/SRÂ 4 in Alma. 6

1930s to 2000s

In February 1934, the eastern terminus of SRÂ 32 was extended east-southeast and southeast to SRÂ 38 in Patterson. In the first quarter of 1937, the eastern terminus was extended again, east-southeast to USÂ 341/SRÂ 27 in Sterling. At the end of 1940, the western terminus was extended north-northwest on USÂ 41/SRÂ 7 to Ashburn and then west-northwest to Dawson. In the second quarter of 1941, the crossing of the Flint River, east of Leesburg, was indicated to have "no bridge or ferry". Between June 1955 and July 1957, this crossing was no longer indicated to have not bridge or ferry. The entire length of SRÂ 32 was paved. In 1993, a western bypass of Leesburg, designated as SRÂ 910, was proposed from USÂ 19/SRÂ 3, where they met SRÂ 32 just south of the city to USÂ 19/SRÂ 3 north-northwest of it. In 1998, SRÂ 3 Byp. was established on the proposed path of SRÂ 910, including the concurrency with SRÂ 32. In 2007, USÂ 19 Byp. was established on the paths of SRÂ 3 Byp. and SRÂ 32 in Leesburg. 7
