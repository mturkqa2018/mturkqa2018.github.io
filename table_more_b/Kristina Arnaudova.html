Kristina Arnaudova (Macedonian: ÐšÑ€Ð¸ÑÑ‚Ð¸Ð½Ð° ÐÑ€Ð½Ð°ÑƒÐ´Ð¾Ð²Ð°; born 21 September 1979 in Negotino, SFR Yugoslavia) is a pop singer from North Macedonia. 1

The promotion of Kristina's debut album "Vozovi" ("Trains") took place on "Mr. Jack Cafe'", Skopje. The album consists of 11 tracks (along with 3 bonus tracks), and is composed by various artists including Kristina herself. Songs on the album include "Znaes koj e toj", "Podaj mi raka", "Vozovi" as well as the very popular song "Prasina" which became a huge hit on the Macedonian music charts. 2

The Maxi Single "Prasina" ("Dust") was the first album in Kristina's career. Alongside the lead track, there are 6 more songs on the album; songs that Kristina recorded with her former band Big Bang as well as the big hit "Znaes koj e toj" which was a Radio and TV favourite during the summer of 2001. On 14 February 2008 on "Hard Rock Discotheque", Kristina promoted her new material, an album of twelve songs entitled "2007". 3
