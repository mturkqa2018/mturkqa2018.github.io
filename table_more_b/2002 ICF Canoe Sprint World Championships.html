2002 ICF Canoe Sprint World Championships

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

The 2002 ICF Canoe Sprint World Championships were held in Seville, Spain. 1

The men's competition consisted of nine Canadian (single paddle, open boat) and nine kayak events. Women competed in nine events, all in kayak. 2

This was the 32nd championships in canoe sprint. 3

Doping controversy

Dmitiry Sabin of the Ukraine won the silver in the C-1 200 m event, but was disqualified when he tested positive for doping. Sabin became the first person to fail a doping test in canoe sprint at an Olympic or world championship level though he did compete at the following year's world championships. 4
