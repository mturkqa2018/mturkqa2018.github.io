Bristol Township is the smallest township in Kendall County, Illinois. As of the 2010 census, its population was 26,230 and it contained 9,229 housing units. 1

Geography

According to the 2010 census, the township has a total area of 28.7 square miles (74Â km), of which 28.31 square miles (73.3Â km) (or 98.64%) is land and 0.39 square miles (1.0Â km) (or 1.36%) is water. 2

U.S. Route 30 and U.S. Route 34 run east to west through the township. Yorkville covers much of the township. 3

The township is named after the Bristol family. 4

Government

The township is governed by an elected Town Board of a Supervisor and four Trustees. The Township also has an elected Assessor, Clerk, and Highway Commissioner. 5
