LDS Conference Center

As of January 1, 2016, The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) reported 2,090,402 members living in Utah in 592 stakes, one district, 5,110 congregations (4,785 wards and 325 branches), and eleven missions. As of January 2019, there are 20 temples operating, under construction, or announced in Utah. 1

Membership history

2

Utah LDS membership

Though the LDS Church membership in Utah has increased, the percentage of Utahns who are Latter-day Saints has declined. Much of this is due to the rapid growth of the state. In 2008, the US Census Bureau determined Utah to be the fastest growing state in the country in terms of population growth. 3

Missions

Due to nonmembers coming into the state, Utah officially became a mission field with its own headquarters in 1975 when the Utah Salt Lake City Mission was organized. Previously, full-time missionaries worked in parts of Utah under the leadership of missions headquartered in other states. 4

Utah currently has 9 missions and a Missionary Training Center. 5
