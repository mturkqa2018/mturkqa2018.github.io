MAN NL262 (A27) is a low-floor single-decker bus made by MAN from 1992 to 1998. Although predominantly produced for Hong Kong in right-hand drive operated by Citybus, some were made in left-hand drive for the mainland European market, it was still built within the similar MAN NL202. It was succeeded by the MAN Lion's City. 2

It has a rounded roof dome similar to the MAN NL202 with a double-curvature windscreen with a separate destination blind and a full low floor layout similar to the MAN NL202 that its rear seats are mounted on platforms. 3

Operations

It is currently in operation with New Lantau Bus and DBTSL in Hong Kong after they were notable as second hand buses, former Citybus buses. 4

Hong Kong

Citybus

Citybus is the launcher of this bus. All 80 buses all in total were ordered as 1501-1580. 5

Two NL262s were written off by fire and traffic accident. The first one is (#1512) was destroyed by fire on 1 November 2008, at Island Eastern Corridor on its way back to the Depot and withdrawn prematurely in 8 May 2009. The second one is (#1560) was damaged by a traffic accident on 17 January 2011, at the branch road near the Hong Kong's side exit at Western Harbour Crossing and withdrawn prematurely in 21 February 2011. 6

New Lantau Bus

5 buses all in total (ex-Citybus 1576-1580). They were allocated to routes 37, 38. 7

DBTSL

10 buses all in total (5 were second hand buses, former Citybus 1570-1574). They were allocated to routes 4, 7, 9, 9A, DB01R and DB03R. 8
