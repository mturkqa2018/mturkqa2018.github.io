Ekaterina Yevgenyevna Alexandrova (Russian: Ð•ÐºÐ°Ñ‚ÐµÑ€Ð¸Ð½Ð° Ð•Ð²Ð³ÐµÐ½ÑŒÐµÐ²Ð½Ð° ÐÐ»ÐµÐºÑÐ°Ð½Ð´Ñ€Ð¾Ð²Ð°; born 15 November 1994) is a Russian professional tennis player. 1

Alexandrova has won two WTA 125K series titles and seven singles titles on the ITF Women's Circuit. On 18 February 2019, she reached her best singles ranking of No. 64. She made her WTA singles debut at the 2016 Katowice Open, where she qualified for the main draw. 2

The Russian made it for the first time into the Grand Slam main draw at the 2016 Wimbledon Championships. In the first round, she defeated Ana Ivanovic in straight sets, after enduring two marathon matches in the qualifying. There she beat Stephanie Vogt and Harriet Dart in three sets. Overall, she played 108 games in just three matches. 3
