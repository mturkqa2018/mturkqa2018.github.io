American singer and actress Selena Gomez has appeared in music videos, films, and television shows. From her debut album with her former band Selena Gomez & the Scene, Kiss & Tell (2009), she released music videos for the singles "Falling Down" and "Naturally". The band's second album, A Year Without Rain (2010), released the music video for "Round & Round" and for the "eponymous single". The band's third album, When the Sun Goes Down (2011), released the music video for "Who Says", "Love You like a Love Song", which was nominated for Best Female Video at the 2012 MTV Video Music Awards, and "Hit the Lights". Early 2012, Gomez announced the band would take a hiatus while Gomez focused on her acting career. In 2013, she confirmed that she would be releasing her solo debut album, as opposed to another album with her band. 1

Gomez's first solo album Stars Dance (2013) released the music video for "Come & Get It", which won the Best Pop Video at the 2013 MTV Video Music Awards, and "Slow Down". Having spent seven years with Hollywood Records, Gomez signed a recording contract with Interscope Records in 2014. To officially end her contract with Hollywood Records, Gomez released the compilation album For You (2014) and released music videos for the single "The Heart Wants What It Wants". Gomez's second studio album Revival (2015) produced the music videos for "Good for You" and "Same Old Love", in 2015, "Hands to Myself" and "Kill Em with Kindness", in 2016. 2

Gomez made her television debut in the children's television series Barney & Friends. From 2007 to 2012, Gomez starred in the Disney Channel television series Wizards of Waverly Place as Alex Russo. Gomez's acting credits include starring roles in the films Another Cinderella Story (2008), Princess Protection Program (2009), Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie (2009), Ramona and Beezus (2010), Monte Carlo (2011), Spring Breakers (2012), Getaway (2013) and The Fundamentals of Caring (2016). She voices the character of Mavis in the Hotel Transylvania film franchise. In 2018, her cumulative box office is around $2.5 billion. 3
