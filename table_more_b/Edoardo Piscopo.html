Edoardo Piscopo (born 4 February 1988) is an Italian racing driver. 1

Career

Formula BMW

After previously competing in karting, Piscopo began his single seater career in Formula BMW USA in 2005, where despite missing the first rounds he finished fifth with three wins for EuroInternational. He also finished fifth in the Bahrain World Final. 2

Formula Renault

After winning in Formula BMW USA Piscopo joined the RED BULL Junior Team and moved back to Europe in 2006 to compete in Formula Renault. In his debut season, he scored ten podiums finishing third in Formula Renault 2.0 Italia for Cram Competition, behind Dani Clos and teammate Adrian Zaugg. He also finished tenth in Eurocup Formula Renault 2.0 for the team. 3

Formula Three

Piscopo stepped up to Formula Three in 2007, competing in the Formula Three Euroseries for MÃ¼cke Motorsport. He only managed to score eight points in 20 races, finishing 15th in the standings. He did however finish fifth in the 2007 Masters of Formula 3 at Zolder. 4

He joined A1 Team Italy to drive in the A1 Grand Prix series for the 2007â€“08 season, sharing duties with Enrico Toccacelo. 5

He moved to the Italian Formula Three Championship in 2008, scoring seven wins on his way to second in the final standings, behind champion Mirko Bortolotti. They were both given the chance to test for Formula One team Scuderia Ferrari. 6

FIA Formula Two Championship

Piscopo became the last driver to sign up for the inaugural season of the FIA Formula Two Championship in 2009. He finished twelfth in the championship due to missing the final round to concentrate on his GP2 Asia campaign. 7

GP2 Series

Piscopo missed the final round of the Formula Two season as he joined DAMS for the 2009â€“10 GP2 Asia Series season. 8

Piscopo made his Main Series dÃ©but for the Trident team at Monza in 2010. He replaced Johnny Cecotto Jr. He was replaced by Federico Leo at the next round of the championship. 9

Auto GP

Piscopo moved to the Auto GP series for 2010. He led the majority of the championship, but was ultimately overhauled by Romain Grosjean. 10

Blancpain Endurance Series

Piscopo was appointed by Lotus as factory driver for the Lotus GT4 campaign. Despite winning 3 rounds out of 5, he finished second in the championship. 11

Porsche Carrera Cup Italia

Piscopo joined the highly competitive Porsche Carrera Cup. He won the best rookie title and finished third in the championship. 12

Lamborghini Super Trofeo

Piscopo has won the European, the American and the World Championship Super Trofeo title. In 2015 he became the Huracan GT3 factory test driver. 13
