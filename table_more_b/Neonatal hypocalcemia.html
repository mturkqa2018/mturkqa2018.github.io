Neonatal hypocalcemia
SpecialtyNeonatology

Neonatal hypocalcemia is an abnormal clinical and laboratory hypocalcemia condition that is frequently observed in infants.[1] 1

Healthy term infants go through a physiological nadir of serum calcium levels at 7.5 - 8.5 mg/dL by day 2 of life. Hypocalcemia is a low blood calcium level. A total serum calcium of less than 8 mg/dL (2mmol/L) or ionized calcium less than 1.2 mmol/L in term neonates is defined as hypocalcemia. In preterm infants, it is defined as less than 7mg/dL (1.75 mmol/L) total serum calcium or less than 4mg/dL (1 mmol/L) ionized calcium. [2] 2

Both early onset hypocalcemia (presents within 72h of birth) and late onset hypocalcemia (presents in 3-7 days after birth) require calcium supplementation treatment. 3

Cause

Risk factors of early neonatal hypocalcemia 4

  • Prematurity
  • Perinatal asphyxia
  • Diabetes mellitus in the mother
  • Maternal hyperparathyroidism
  • Intrauterine growth retardation (IUGR)
  • Iatrogenic

Risk factors

Risk factors of late neonatal hypocalcemia 5

  • Exogenous phosphate load
  • Use of gentamicin
  • Gender and ethnic: late neonatal hypocalcemia occurred more often in male infants and Hispanic infants
  • Others
  • Magnesium deficiency
  • Transient hypoparathyroidism of newborn
  • Hypoparathyroidism due to other causes (DiGeorge Syndrome)
