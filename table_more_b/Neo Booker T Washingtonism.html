Neo Booker T Washingtonism is a continued conceptualization of Booker T Washington's position in the 1960s civil rights movement of assuaging race relations between white and black Americans by positing that blacks should postpone their aspirations of full civil rights and work within the system of the government to ultimately be accepted in the long run as productive citizens. 1
