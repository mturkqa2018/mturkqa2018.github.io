Route description

1

Eastbound on NYÂ 295 in Chatham between the Taconic Parkway and I-90

At its western terminus, NYÂ 295 begins at NYÂ 66 in the village of Chatham, initially known as Railroad Avenue. As the road changes names to Spring Street, the Stony Kill approaches from the west and roughly parallels NYÂ 295 for much of its duration. Within the hamlet, NYÂ 295 intersects several local roads and a pair of train tracks. The highway proceeds towards the northwest as it passes to the east of a pond, and subsequently intersects the Taconic State Parkway. Upon crossing the state parkway, NYÂ 295 takes a more easterly direction; however, it turns towards the northwest when it reaches New Concord Road. The road intersects the Berkshire Connector of the New York State Thruway, or InterstateÂ 90 (I-90), and enters the hamlet of East Chatham. 3

In East Chatham, NYÂ 295 becomes concurrent with County RouteÂ 9. When the two highways split, NYÂ 295 heads towards the east. Several local highways intersect from the north, while the highway remains routed along the Stony Kill. The road bends towards the southeast into Canaan, where the Stony Kill turns sharply towards the north and NYÂ 295 crosses the Queechy Lake Brook. Crossing between two mountains, the highway passes to the southwest of Queechy Lake, and intersects with NYÂ 22. After a final, brief turn to the east, NYÂ 295 crosses the New Yorkâ€“Massachusetts border; it continues into Massachusetts as RouteÂ 295, a connector road between NYÂ 295 and RouteÂ 41 before terminating. 4
