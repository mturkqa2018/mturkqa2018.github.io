The Ch'ÅngnyÅn Ich'Ån Line is an electrified standard-gauge secondary mainline of the Korean State Railway running from P'yÅngsan on the P'yÅngbu Line to Sep'o on the KangwÅn Line. The 141.3Â km (87.8Â mi) line is the southernmost of the three east-west transversal mainlines in North Korea. 2

The Ch'ÅngnyÅn Ich'Ån line plays an important role in the movement of freight between the east and west coasts of the DPRK, relieving the burden on the P'yÅngra Line. History

As freight movements between the east and west coasts of the DPRK grew in the years after the end of the Korean War, congestion on the P'yÅngra Line became a major issue. To relieve this, the Korean State Railway undertook the construction of a third, more southern transversal line through the mountainous south-central region of the DPRK. Work began in 1957, and the first section to be completed was the 51.4Â km (31.9Â mi) section from P'yÅngsan to Chihari, which was opened to traffic in 1962, whilst the rest of the route, 89.9Â km (55.9Â mi) from Chihari to Sep'o Ch'ÅngnyÅn, was opened in October 1972. 5

Electrification of the line was completed in 1980. 6

Services

Both passenger and freight trains operate on the Ch'ÅngnyÅn Ich'Ån Line, with freight being considerably more significant. 7

Freight

In order to reduce congestion on the P'yÅngra Line, much transit freight is routed via the Ch'ÅngnyÅn Ich'Ån Line. Steel products from the Hwanghae Iron & Steel Complex in Songrim on the Songrim Line and cement from the February 8 Cement Complex in West Pongsan on the P'yÅngbu Line make up a major portion of eastbound through traffic. 8

Westbound traffic is 1.6 to 1.9 times greater than eastbound. Westbound, the primary commodities shipped are ore (18.4âˆ’18.6%), wood (16.3%), fertiliser (12âˆ’13.1%), and marine products (7.3âˆ’7.5%); these four commodities make up roughly 56% of westbound cargo. Over half - 50âˆ’60% - of eastbound cargo is grain; ores (8.6âˆ’11.2%), metals (9.9âˆ’10.7%), and cement (3.2âˆ’3.7%) accounts for much of the rest. Grain from the rich granary of Hwanghae is shipped east exclusively along this line, whilst the westbound ore is primarily magnetite from the TÅksÅng and Musan Mining Complexes destined for the Hwanghae Iron & Steel Complex on the Songrim Line. There is also a uranium concentrate plant at P'yÅngsan. 9
