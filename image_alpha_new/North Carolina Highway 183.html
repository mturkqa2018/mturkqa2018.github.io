North Carolina Highway 183 (NCÂ 183) is a primary state highway in the U.S. state of North Carolina. It traverses from USÂ 221, in the community of Linville Falls, to NCÂ 181, near the community of Jonas Ridge. 2

Route description

1

First NCÂ 183 West sign, near Jonas Ridge

The route serves mainly as a connector between Linville Falls and the rest of Burke County; it also serves as an alternate route to the Blue Ridge Parkway, which parallels the route. The route also has access to the Linville Falls and Gorge Wilderness Area. Part of the route is shared with NC Bike Route 2. 3

Where NCÂ 183 begins, it is flanked by nearby McDowell and Avery counties. The highway also crosses briefly into McDowell county twice, in two short successions, in Linville Falls. Nearly halfway along the highway, a spur road from the Blue Ridge Parkway does a fly-over above the highway (no access). 4

NCÂ 183, with continuation via NCÂ 181, can be considered an alternate route of USÂ 221, bypassing Altamont and Crossnore. 5

Scenic byways

NCÂ 183 is part of one scenic byway in the state (indicated by a Scenic Byways sign). 6

Pisgah Loop Scenic Byway is a 47-mile (76Â km) loop byway. The route is not recommended for recreational vehicles or buses, and four-wheel-drive vehicles are required for unpaved portions. It is noted for its close proximity to the Blue Ridge Parkway, the Linville River and Falls, and a rare montane (mountain wetland). NCÂ 183 makes up the northern segment of the loop, which also includes Old NC Highway 105 (SRÂ 1238), NCÂ 126, Fish Hatchery Road (SRÂ 1254/SRÂ 1240), and NCÂ 181. 7

History

NCÂ 183 was established around 1930 as a new primary route between NCÂ 105 and NCÂ 181, in northern Burke County. In 1954, NCÂ 183 replaced part of NCÂ 105, into Linville Falls, to its current western terminus at USÂ 221. 8
