"And the Rock Cried Out, No Hiding Place" is an episode from the third season of the science fiction television series Babylon 5. 2

Synopsis

Ivanova and Brother Theo greet Reverend Will Dexter, Rabbi Leo Meyers, and other religious leaders. Knowing that a full-scale war with the Shadows will break out, they want to help the populace of Babylon 5 with their faith. Delenn arranges for Sheridan to have dinner with the leaders, knowing that he has become too engrossed with trying to plan for the war. There, the leaders promise their help, providing Sheridan with several data crystals of communications from Earth that show that the resistance against President Clark is strong; the religious leaders want to help as they know that fear and ignorance can be used as weapons. Later, Dexter comes to see Sheridan, having recognized his weariness as well as Delenn's feelings towards him. Sheridan curtly asks him to leave but Dexter relates a story of his own wife, and explains that sometimes it helps to share the burden of responsibility. Later, Dexter asks Sheridan if they can hold a service before he leaves, which he agrees. Delenn drags Sheridan off to participate in the ceremony when he gets too in depth in trying to analyze the Shadows' strategy. 3

Ambassador Mollari learns that Lord Refa is arriving at the station along with a high-ranking minister in the Centauri government. He prepares to meet them, but gives Vir Cotto instructions to tell G'Kar that his former aide, Na'Toth, is being held prisoner on the Narn homeworld. Mollari explains that G'Kar will likely find a way to return to Narn to free Na'Toth, but he will have forces ready to apprehend G'Kar, considered a wanted criminal by the Centauri government, which he will use to gain favor with the minister. Agents for Refa see Vir deliver this message to G'Kar, and G'Kar immediately acting on it to seek transit to Narn. Refa arranges for Vir to be abducted and subjected to a telepathic scan, revealing Mollari's plan. Refa decides to travel to Narn directly and take the credit for G'Kar's capture, usurping Mollari's reputation. 4

Refa meets with the Centauri leadership on Narn, and requests a number of guards to help them pursue G'Kar and his forces when they attempt to break into the prison. As Refa expected, G'Kar leads a small group of Narns through the tunnels into the prison, and Refa and the guards surround them. Refa orders the guards to execute the other Narns but they do not react. Instead, G'Kar pulls out a holographic projection crystal with a pre-recorded message from Mollari. Mollari knew Refa would look for any way to gain advantage, and thus arranged the rouse with G'Kar to lure Refa here, as well as assuring the Centauri guards would follow Mollari's orders. Mollari reveals he has the proof that Refa had arranged the murder of the former prime minister, as well as the assault on Narn and directly responsible for millions of Narn deaths. He also accuses Refa of poisoning the love of life, Adira, as well as his best friend due to Refa's manipulations. G'Kar, as instructed, leaves a data crystal on Refa's body to be found with the evidence of these crimes. As Rev. Dexter leads the service in singing the hymn "No Hiding Place Down Here" on Babylon 5, Refa attempts to find a place to hide from the Narn, but is eventually killed. Later, Mollari presents the minister with the data crystal found on Refa's body, assuring his favor. Vir expresses his discontent at being used by Mollari just to gain a foothold for his own personal benefit. 5

Delenn has Sheridan accompany her on the White Star, showing him that the Minbari have been busy creating a whole fleet of White Star ships in preparation of fighting the war. Relieved to have the firepower he needs, Sheridan and Delenn share a kiss. 6

Production details

  • The Gospel song "There's No Hiding Place Down Here" is a traditional Negro spiritual, originally collected in 1907 and first printed in 1915, and popularized in a 1955 recording by Dorothy Love Coates & The Original Gospel Harmonettes. Marva Hicks leads the sermon in this song in the episode.
