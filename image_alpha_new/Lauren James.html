Lauren James (born 29 September 2001) is an English footballer who plays as a forward for FA Women's Championship club Manchester United. 2

Club career

Arsenal

On 29 September 2017, James made her debut for Arsenal, coming on in the 67th minute, replacing Lisa Evans in a 2â0 Arsenal win over Everton in the 2017â18 season. 3

Manchester United

James playing for Manchester United against Brighton & Hove Albion in 2019.

On 13 July 2018, it was announced that James would be part of the first professional Manchester United squad due to compete in the newly restructured 2018â19 FA Women's Championship. She scored the opening two goals in United's first game of the 2018â19 FA Women's Championship season, a 12â0 win away to Aston Villa. James was voted FA Women's Championship player of the month for September following her three goals across Manchester United's unbeaten opening month. 4

International career

In April 2017, James made her under-17 debut in a 2â0 friendly defeat to USA. On October 14, 2017 she captained England during a 10â0 win over Latvia as part of 2018 UEFA Women's Under-17 Championship qualification, scoring four goals. 5

In January 2019, James received her first call-up to the under-19 squad for the Algarve Tournament. 6

Personal life

James is the younger sister of Chelsea academy product and England youth international Reece James. She has cited her father Nigel, a UEFA licensed football coach, as inspiration: "[He] has helped me every step of the way. I grew up playing football with my brothers and wanted to play like them; I have always loved the game. I am grateful to my Dad for all the time, effort and love that he has put into my football by coaching me in order that I reach the very best level." 7

She attended Whitton School. 8
