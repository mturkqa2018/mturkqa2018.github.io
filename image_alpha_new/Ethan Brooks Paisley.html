Biography

Ethan Brooks Paisley was born on August 1, 2000 in Petaluma, California. He began his filmmaking career at the age of twelve when he gained county-wide fame for his humorous YouTube spoof-style series entitled ethanpaisley. 2

In 2015, Paisley wrote and directed The Art of Escape. The film premiered at the Singapore International Film Festival and won distribution with Universal Markets [UK]. The same year, he began his production company Take18 Entertainment. Under Take18 Entertainment, Paisley wrote and directed Point 453 (2016). Point 453 screened at the 70th Cannes Film Festival at the MarchÃ© du Film and was distributed by Adler and Associates Entertainment [US]. 3

Paisley gave a talk at the ieSonoma County conference organized by TED (conference) with Sir Ken Robinson in 2017. Later that year, Paisley was one of 40 high school students who collaborated with Elysium Bandini Studios (co-founded by James Franco) to direct Metamorphosis: Junior High. 4

Throughout his high school career, Paisley produced Indelible (2017) and Wilted (2018). Indelible premiered at the 40th Mill Valley Film Festival and later made Paisley the inaugural recipient of the "Best Young Director" award from the Young Entertainer Awards. The latter will be opening the Nice International Film Festival in 2018. 5

At the Sundance Film Festival (2018), it was announced Paisleyâ€™s script Turn Out will be directed by Indrani and produced by Eric J. Adams of Sleeperwave Films. 6

Ethan has been featured in Essex TV, WELAB Magazine, and The San Francisco Chronicle, among other periodicals. He was nominated as one of Sonoma County's "30 under 30" (2017), and is currently a film student at the Dodge College of Film and Media, Chapman University. 7
