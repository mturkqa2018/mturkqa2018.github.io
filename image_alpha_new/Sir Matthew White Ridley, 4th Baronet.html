Sir Matthew White Ridley, 4th Baronet

Sir Matthew White Ridley, 4th Baronet (9 September 1807 â€“ 25 September 1877) was a Conservative Party politician in the United Kingdom. 1

He was appointed High Sheriff of Northumberland for 1841 and then served as Member of Parliament (MP) for Northumberland North from 1859 to 1868. 2

He inherited the baronetcy on the death in 1836 of his father Matthew, and was in turn succeeded by his son Matthew, who was later ennobled as Viscount Ridley. 3
