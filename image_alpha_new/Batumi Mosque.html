The Batumi Mosque (Georgian: áƒ‘áƒáƒ—áƒ£áƒ›áƒ˜áƒ¡ áƒ›áƒ”áƒ©áƒ”áƒ—áƒ˜, batumis mecheti, Orta Dgzame) is a mosque in Batumi, Adjara, Georgia, which is a home to a sizable Muslim community. It was commissioned by the family of Aslan Beg (the equivalent of duke) Khimshiashvili, a Muslim Georgian nobleman in 1886. The walls of the mosque were painted by the Laz brothers. The mosque is popularly known as the "Jamia in the middle" ("áƒáƒ áƒ—áƒ áƒ¯áƒáƒ›áƒ”", orta jame) for it once stood in between two other mosques which have not survived. 1
