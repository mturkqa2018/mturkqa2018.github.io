The Ranthambore Express is a superfast train service which runs between Indore Junction railway station in Indore, the largest city and the commercial hub of the Central Indian state Madhya Pradesh, and Jodhpur, a major city in Rajasthan, India. It is the important train for Indore city as it connects Indore to the Capital of MARWAR and cultural city of India called suryanagri ( Jodhpur) 3

It was earlier known as Intercity Express. 4

Number and nomenclature

The number allowed for the train is: 5

  • 12465 - Indore to Jodhpur
  • 12466 - Jodhpur to Indore

The name "Ranthambore Express" refers to the forest of Rajasthan state named Ranthambore National Park 6

Arrival and departure information

The train operates daily in each direction. 7

  • Train no.12465 departs from Indore daily at 06:00 hrs. and arrives at Jodhpur the same day at 22:15 hrs.
  • Train no.12466 departs from Jodhpur at 06:10 and arrives at Indore the same day at 22:40 hrs.

Routes and halts

The train goes via Ujjain, Nagda and Jaipur, Kota. The important halts of the train are : 8

INDORE JUNCTION → Dewas → Ujjain Junction → Nagda Junction (rake reversal ) → Vikramgarh → Ramganj Mandi railway station → Kota Junction → Sawai Madhopur ( rake reversal & diesel loco augmented till Jodhpur )→ JAIPUR → Makrana Junction → Degana → Jodhpur railway station 9

Locomotive links and coach composite

The train is hauled by BRC WAP4/WAM4 locomotives from Indore to Sawai Madhopur and from there by BGKT WDP4/4B. It consists of 14/15 coaches: 10

  • 3 AC 3 Tier
  • 1 AC Chair Car
  • 2 Sleeper
  • 3 Reserved Chair Car
  • 6 General
  • 1 SLR cum Ladies
  • 1 SLR cum Handicap BRAKE CUM LUGGAGE / PARCEL VAN
