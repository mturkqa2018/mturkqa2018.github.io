Grevillea corrugata is a shrub which is endemic to an area near Bindoon in the south west of Western Australia. It grows to between 0.5 and 1.5 metres in height . 2

It has deeply lobed leaves. The ultimate lobes have curved margins and are 1.5 to 3Â cm long and 0.7 to 0.8Â mm wide. The white or cream flowers appear from August to September (late winter to early spring) in its native range. 3

The species was first formally described by Peter Olde and Neil Marriott, their description published in Nuytsia in 1993. It is classified as Priority One Flora (Poorly Known) under the Wildlife Conservation Act in Western Australia. 4
