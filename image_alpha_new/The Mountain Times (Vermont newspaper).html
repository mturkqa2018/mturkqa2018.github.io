The Mountain Times is an American newspaper serving Killington, Vermont. It is published each Thursday with a circulation of over 10,000. The paper is owned by Angelo Lynn, Polly Lynn, and Jason Mikula. The newspaper covers Killington and surrounding towns in Rutland County. The current editor is Polly Lynn. 1

History

The paper was founded in 1971 by Ed Pickett as the Killington Pico-Paper. Pickett, an advertising manager for the Mount Snow Ski Resort, also published Ski Racer and Snow Valley News. In 1976, Pickett sold the paper to Andrew Neisner, who changed the name to the The Mountain Times. 2

In 1986, Royal and Zip Barnard bought the The Mountain Times. They operated it for twenty-five years before selling it in 2011, along with eight other local newspapers, to the Lynn family . The sale of the paper in 2011 was delayed by significant flooding caused by Tropical Storm Irene. The basement of the paper's printing office was damaged in the flood, but then-publisher Royal Barnard managed to publish an issue from his home that week. 3

Under the new management, the paper increased revenue by 15 percent by 2013. 4

National coverage

The Mountain Times was the first to report about a proposal for Killington to secede from Vermont and join New Hampshire in 2004. 5
