Twin Fantasy (Face to Face) is the eleventh studio album by American indie rock band Car Seat Headrest, released on February 16, 2018. It is a complete re-recording and reworking of the band's sixth studio album, Twin Fantasy, released in 2011. 1

The CD release is bundled with the original 2011 album. 2

Background and recording

Recording for Twin Fantasy (Face to Face) began in 2016, with much of the instrumental work being recorded live by engineer Adam Stilson at Soundhouse in Seattle. Vocals were recorded separately at Decade Music Studios in Chicago. Post production and mixing took place between Decade Music Studios and Will Toledo's laptop, with most effects, stems and final mixes having run through the studios vintage mixing console. "Stop Smoking (We Love You)" was recorded by Toledo in his apartment. 3

On December 13, 2017, the band released the re-recorded version of "Beach Life-In-Death" on Spotify without prior announcement. This sparked fan rumours that the album would be re-recorded and released the following year. On December 27, 2017, an Amazon listing detailing a re-recorded version of Twin Fantasy was found by fans, and subsequently uploaded to the Car Seat Headrest subreddit. This was followed up by a listing on SRCVinyl.com with the date February 16, 2018, which contained an abridged version of the Matador Records press announcement. 4

On January 9, 2018, Matador Records formally announced the release of the re-recording, entitled Twin Fantasy (Face to Face). The album was released via Matador on February 16. The original, re-titled Twin Fantasy (Mirror to Mirror), was released on vinyl as a part of Record Store Day on April 21. The re-recording's announcement coincided with a tour announcement and a music video for the track "Nervous Young Inhumans". 5

Twin Fantasy (Face to Face) was met with positive reviews from contemporary music critics. On the review aggregate site Metacritic, the album received a score of 87 out of 100 based on 21 reviews, indicating "universal acclaim." Hannah Vettese of Record Collector gave the album a perfect score, calling Toledo's lyrics "exhilarating" and described the album as "no stop-gap, contract filler of a record but rather a perfectionist giving a great album the full workout it deserved." In a review for Pitchfork, Mark Richardson commented that "Toledo pulls off an album with a jarring degree of specificity that touches on feelings familiar to almost anyone who has experienced young desire and heartbreak." Music journalist Robert Christgau gave the album an A-, stating that his favourite track was also the shortest, "Stop Smoking". 6
