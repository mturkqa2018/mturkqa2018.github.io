Aftercharge is a team-based cooperative multiplayer online first-person shooter game developed and published by Chainsawesome Games. It was released on January 10, 2019 for Windows and Xbox One, with a Nintendo Switch version planned at a later date.The first public testing phase began and lasted through October 13â€“15, 2017. 1

Gameplay

Aftercharge assigns players into two teams of three, pitting invisible robots against an invincible security squad in tactical skirmishes, with each player placed on either the attacking side or the defending side. The attackers take the role of invisible robots and will make their way to destroy energy extractors located on the map, which are the main objective of the game. The defenders take the role of guards (dubbed enforcers) and will attempt to shut down the robots to protect the extractors. Unlike most first-person shooters, Aftercharge does not include the class system in most games. Instead, players choose from a character pool on each side with widely different abilities and roles to vary their team's strategy. Players on a team work together to destroy or defend control points on a map in a limited amount of time. Players also gain cosmetic rewards that do not affect gameplay, such as character and weapon skins, as they play the game. Aftercharge features squad-based combat with two opposing teams of three players each. Six glowing structures dubbed extractors are at the center of the gameplay, are spread out across the map and serve as the objective for both teams. The attackers have to coordinate their attacks, create distractions and sneak around to destroy them while the enforcers have to cover as much ground as possible and use their abilities to spot the attackers and stop them before they can destroy all of the extractors. Players choose one of the several characters, each with their own unique abilities and roles. Players will take either the role of the defending side, which are guards, or the attacking side composed of invisible robots. Once an attacker's health has been depleted, it becomes "disabled" being able to see but not use any abilities or move. A disabled attacker can be revived by their teammates, making the game a lot more cooperative, for if all of the attackers are disabled at once, the defenders win the match. 2
