State RouteÂ 110 (SRÂ 110) is a 11.10-mile-long (17.86Â km) state highway serving Olympic National Park and the Quileute Indian Reservation within Clallam County, located in the U.S. state of Washington. The highway travels west from U.S. RouteÂ 101 (USÂ 101) in Forks as La Push Road along the Quileute and Sol Duc rivers to the boundary of Olympic National Park east of La Push. La Push Road was originally added to the state highway system in 1937 as Secondary State HighwayÂ 9B (SSHÂ 9B), but was removed in 1955. SRÂ 110 was later established on the route in 1991 after a previous highway with the same designation was removed. A spur route extending west to Mora was originally added with SSHÂ 9B and re-added with SRÂ 110 in 1991. 2

Route description

SRÂ 110 begins as La Push Road at an intersection with USÂ 101 in Forks, located between the Sol Duc and Calawah rivers. The highway travels southwest towards the Pacific Ocean, intersecting its spur route to Mora before crossing the Bogachiel River and ending at the entrance to Olympic National Park. La Push Road continues northwest into the Quileute Indian Reservation and the community of La Push. 3

Every year, the Washington State Department of Transportation (WSDOT) conducts a series of surveys on its highways in the state to measure traffic volume. This is expressed in terms of average annual daily traffic (AADT), which is a measure of traffic volume for any average day of the year. In 2011, WSDOT calculated that between 1,300 and 2,300 vehicles per day used the highway, mostly at the USÂ 101 intersection. 4

History

SRÂ 110 was established in 1991 on the existing La Push Road from USÂ 101 in Forks to Olympic National Park, previously part of SSHÂ 9B. SSHÂ 9B and a spur route to Mora was designated in 1937 as part of the newly created Primary and secondary state highways, but was removed in 1955. After the 1964 highway renumbering, a separate SRÂ 110 was established as an auxiliary route of SRÂ 11, extending from the highway in Fairhaven to InterstateÂ 5 (I-5) in Bellingham. SRÂ 11 replaced SRÂ 110 after being re-aligned to end at I-5 in 1975. SRÂ 110 was designated as the Operations Desert Shield and Desert Storm Memorial Highway by the state legislature and state transportation commission in 2010. 5

SRÂ 110 has a 2.67-mile-long (4.30Â km) spur route serving Mora to the north of the Quileute River. The highway, known as Mora Road, begins at SRÂ 110 west of Forks and travels west across the Sol Duc River into Olympic National Park and the Quileute Indian Reservation, crossing the Dickey River into Mora. The gravel road was added to the state highway system as a spur of SSHÂ 9B in 1937, but the highways were removed in 1955. After SRÂ 110 was re-added to the state highway system in 1991, the spur route was put into state maintenance once again. 7
