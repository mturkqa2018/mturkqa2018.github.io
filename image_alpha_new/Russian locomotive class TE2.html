The TE2 (Cyrillic script: Ð¢Ð­2) is a class of Soviet diesel-electric locomotives built by Kharkov in Ukraine from 1948 to 1955. It is nominally a two-unit version of class TE1 (Ð¢Ð­1) but is very different in appearance. While the TE1 is a Co-Co hood unit, the TE2 is a Bo-Bo+Bo-Bo cab unit. 1

Powertrain

Each of the two units is powered by a 736Â kW (987Â hp; 1,001Â PS) Penza D50 4-stroke 6 cylinder supercharged diesel engine and has four 152Â kW (204Â hp; 207Â PS) DC traction motors. 2

Production

Prototypes were built in 1948 and 1949 and series production ran from 1950 to 1955. A total of 528 pairs was built. Each pair carried a single number in the range 001 to 528. 3

Preservation

TE2-414 is preserved at the Museum of Railway Technology, Saint Petersburg. 4

TE4

The TE4 (Ð¢Ð­4) was an experimental modification of a TE2, designed to run on solid fuel by using a producer gas system. The gas generator was mounted on a four-axle non-powered unit, placed between the TE2 power units, making a wheel arrangement of Bo-Bo+2-2+Bo-Bo. It was not a success and was converted back to a TE2. See Ð¢Ð­4 at the Russian-language Wikipedia. 5

TE6

The TE6 (Ð¢Ð­6) was a class of sixteen TE2-type locomotives ordered by the Ministry of Defence in 1952. They were single-section and their purpose was to serve as locomotives or mobile alternating current generators. They were designed to "meet the requirements of protection against injury factors of a nuclear explosion". Ð¢Ð­6 [1] 6
