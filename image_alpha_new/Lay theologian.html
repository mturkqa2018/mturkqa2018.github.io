A lay theologian is a theologian who has not received formal theological training. Many who are regarded as lay theologians have advanced degrees in other academic disciplines. 1

Among lay theologians are the following: 2

  • George Abbot
  • Ethan Allen
  • Joseph T. Bayly
  • Edward Musgrave Blaiklock
  • Nicholas Cabasilas
  • G. K. Chesterton
  • James F. Colaianni
  • Jacques Ellul
  • Thomas Erskine
  • Fyodor Dostoevsky
  • Jim Forest
  • Nicolas Gomez Davila
  • Rosemary Haughton
  • Aleksey Khomyakov
  • C. S. Lewis
  • Robert Magliola
  • Isaac Newton
  • Johann Heinrich Pabst
  • Caspar Peucer
  • Dorothy L. Sayers
  • Frank Sheed
  • Uwe Siemon-Netto
  • William Stringfellow
  • Maisie Ward
  • John Lennox
  • Slavoj Zizek
