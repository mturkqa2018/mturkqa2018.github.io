Krems an der Donau (German pronunciation: [kÊÉ›ms an dÉ›ÉÌ¯ ËˆdoËnaÊŠÌ¯]) is a town of 23,992 inhabitants in Austria, in the federal state of Lower Austria. It is the fifth-largest city of Lower Austria and is approximately 70 kilometres (43 miles) west of Vienna. Krems is a city with its own statute (or Statutarstadt), and therefore it is both a municipality and a district. 1

Geography

Krems is located at the confluence of the Krems and Danube Rivers at the eastern end of Wachau valley, in the southern Waldviertel. 2

Krems borders the following municipalities: Stratzing, Langenlois, Rohrendorf bei Krems, Gedersdorf, Traismauer, NuÃŸdorf ob der Traisen, Paudorf, Furth bei GÃ¶ttweig, Mautern an der Donau, DÃ¼rnstein, and Senftenberg. 3

Krems was first mentioned in 995 in a certificate of Otto III, but settlement was apparent even before then. For example, a child's grave, over 27,000 years old, was found here. This is the oldest grave found in Austria. 4

During the 11th and 12th centuries, Chremis, as it was then called, was almost as large as Vienna. 5

Krems is the primary producer of Marillenschnaps, an apricot brandy. 6

Krems is the hometown of Martin Johann Schmidt, called "Kremserschmidt", the leading painter, draughtsman and etcher of the Austrian late Baroque. 7
