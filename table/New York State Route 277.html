Route description

North Boston to West Seneca

NYÂ 277 begins at an intersection with NYÂ 391 (Boston State Road) in the hamlet of North Boston (in the town of Boston). Running northeast along Herman Hill Road, NYÂ 277 is a two-lane local road, passing the North Boston Stadium, crossing a junction with South Abbott Road (County RouteÂ 28 or CRÂ 28) before becoming a dense woods road into the town of Orchard Park and changing names to Boston Ridge Road. Returning to a residential road, the route turns eastward south Chestnut Ridge Park, reaching a junction with Chestnut Ridge Road (CRÂ 552), where NYÂ 277 turns northward. 3

1

NYÂ 277 north of NYÂ 391 in North Boston

Running along the southeastern edge of Chestnut Ridge Park, NYÂ 277 becomes a two-lane residential street through Orchard Park, crossing north past Gartman Road (CRÂ 529). Continuing north, the route crosses into Chestnut Ridge Park, reaching an interchange with Sheriff Drive in the park. A short distance north of Sheriff Drive, the route leaves the park, reaching the eastern terminus of Newton Road (CRÂ 110). Returning to the residential neighborhoods, NYÂ 277 bypasses the hamlet of Ellicott, which is connected via Powers Road (CRÂ 370). Just south of the border with the village of Orchard Park, NYÂ 277 reaches the hamlet of Duells Corner, where the route junctions with NYÂ 240 (New Armor Duells Road). 4

Now in the village of Orchard Park, NYÂ 277 and NYÂ 240 become concurrent, passing west of the Orchard Park Country Club as South Buffalo Street. Passing east of Yates Park and Green Lake, the routes cross under a railroad line owned by Buffalo and Pittsburgh Railroad and soon reach the center of Orchard Park. The routes pass west of Woodlawn Cemetery, becoming a two-lane commercial street into a junction with USÂ 20A (Quaker Street). Now known as North Buffalo Road, NYÂ 277 and NYÂ 240 continue north, crossing through the village as a two-lane commercial road. Crossing back into the town of Orchard Park, the routes enter the hamlet of Webster Corners and intersect with the termini of CRÂ 103 and CRÂ 369. 5

A short distance to the north, the routes, now a residential road, cross a junction with Milestrip Road (CRÂ 460), the county-maintained continuation of NYÂ 179. Now known as Orchard Park Road, NYÂ 277 and NYÂ 240 continue north through Orchard Park before reaching a junction with USÂ 20 (Southwestern Boulevard) in front of a large strip mall. Just north of the junction with USÂ 20, NYÂ 240 and NYÂ 277 enter Harold Square and split at a fork, with NYÂ 240 running northwest along Orchard Park and NYÂ 277 running north on Union Road. NYÂ 277 soon crosses into the town of West Seneca and becomes a four-lane residential street. 6

West Seneca to Amherst

NYÂ 277 crosses north past a junction with Reserve Road (CRÂ 366) and East and West Road (CRÂ 363) before passing the Houghton College at West Seneca. NYÂ 277 then crosses over Cazenovia Creek and enters the commercial hamlet of Ebenezer, where it intersects with Seneca Street (CRÂ 215). Continuing north through the town of West Seneca, passing east of Centennial Park and entering a junction with NYÂ 16 (Center Road). Passing Mount Hope Cemetery, the route goes north several blocks into an interchange with NYÂ 400 (the Aurora Expressway) before crossing over tracks owned by Norfolk Southern. 7

2

NYÂ 277 at the junction with NYÂ 16 in West Seneca

After the tracks, NYÂ 277 becomes an industrial roadway along Union Road, before entering the hamlet of Gardenville, where it intersects with the eastern terminus of Indian Church Road (CRÂ 534) near the Charles Burchfield Nature and Art Center. After crossing over Buffalo Creek, NYÂ 277 reaches a junction with NYÂ 354 (Clinton Street). NYÂ 277 continues north along Union Road through Gardenville as a four-lane residential street. The route soon enters the town of Cheektowaga, where it becomes a four-lane industrial street near French Road (CRÂ 321) through south Cheektowaga. The route soon becomes a residential boulevard as it winds north through Cheektowaga, crossing another creek into the Edgebrook Estates neighborhood. 8

Running north through Edgebrook Estates, NYÂ 277 passes a long strip mall on the northbound side, soon crossing under tracks owned by Norfolk Southern, crossing into the Bellvue section of Cheektowaga. Crossing a grade-separated interchange with NYÂ 130 (Broadway), the route crosses under tracks owned by CSX Transportation and used by Amtrak, just west of the Buffalo-Depew Amtrak station. NYÂ 277 continues north through Cheektowaga, crossing a junction with Walden Avenue (NYÂ 952Q), an unsigned reference route. The route then runs east of the entrances to the Walden Galleria and crosses through the commercial Maryvale section of Cheektowaga. 9

NYÂ 277 southbound from NYÂ 324 in Amherst

North of an abandoned railroad grade, NYÂ 277 and Union Road become a four-lane residential boulevard through Maryvale, crossing a junction with Genesee Street and soon into an interchange with NYÂ 33 (the Kensington Expressway). North of the Kensington, NYÂ 277 remains a four-lane boulevard through U-Crest and Maryvale, passing numerous residences on its way north into the town of Amherst. After crossing a junction with Wehrle Drive (CRÂ 290), NYÂ 277, known now as South Union Road, crosses under the New York State Thruway (I-90) just east of exitÂ 50 and west of the Williamsville toll barrier, now in the village of Williamsville. 10

In Williamsville, NYÂ 277 remains a four-lane residential boulevard, crossing a junction with NYÂ 5 (Main Street). At this junction, NYÂ 277 becomes known as North Union Road, reducing to two lanes as it passes more residences in Williamsville. Just north of North Forest Park, the route turns northwest and returns to the town of Amherst. Now known as North Forest Road, the route winds northwest past multiple residences before reaching a junction with NYÂ 324 (Sheridan Drive). The junction with NYÂ 324 marks the northern terminus of NYÂ 277, while North Forest Road continues north as CRÂ 294 past Westwood Country Club. 11

History

In the 1930 renumbering of state highways in New York, the portion of Union Road between NYÂ 354 in Gardenville and Genesee Street (then NYÂ 33) in Cheektowaga was designated as part of NYÂ 355, a route that continued north to Williamsville by way of Genesee Street and Cayuga Road. At the same time, the section of Union Road in northern Orchard Park was designated as part of NYÂ 240. NYÂ 277, meanwhile, was assigned by the following year to the portion of its modern routing from Boston north to NYÂ 240 south of the village of Orchard Park. 12

The Union Road portion of NYÂ 355 was incorporated into NYÂ 18B c.â€‰1935. The suffixed route was an alternate route of NYÂ 18 that began at NYÂ 240 in Orchard Park and followed what is now NYÂ 277 north to Sheridan Drive (NYÂ 324). From there, NYÂ 18B followed NYÂ 324 west to Bailey Avenue, where it connected to NYÂ 18. NYÂ 355 was truncated northward to the eastern terminus of its overlap with NYÂ 33 as a result. The NYÂ 355 designation was completely removed c.â€‰1937. NYÂ 18 was truncated to Lewiston on its western end on January 1, 1962, leading to the removal of the NYÂ 18B designation. The portion of the route's former alignment that did not overlap NYÂ 324 became a northward extension of NYÂ 277. 13
