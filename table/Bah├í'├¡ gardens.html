The Terraces of the Bah√°'√≠ Faith, also known as the Hanging Gardens of Haifa, are garden terraces around the Shrine of the B√°b on Mount Carmel in Haifa, Israel. Fariborz Sahba began work in 1987 designing the gardens and oversaw construction. The terraces were opened to the public in June 2001. Beginning at its base, the gardens extend almost a kilometre up the side of Mount Carmel, covering some 200,000 square metres of land. The gardens are linked by a set of stairs flanked by twin streams of running water cascading down the mountainside through the steps and terrace bridges. 3
