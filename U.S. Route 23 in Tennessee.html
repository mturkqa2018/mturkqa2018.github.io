U.S. RouteÂ 23 (USÂ 23) is a part of the U.S. Highway System that travels from Jacksonville, Florida to Mackinaw City, Michigan. In the U.S. state of Tennessee, the U.S. Highway travels 57.5 miles (92.5Â km) from the North Carolina state line, at Sam's Gap, north to the Virginia state line, in Kingsport. With a predominant concurrency with Interstate 26 (I-26), USÂ 23 is a divided four-lane freeway that follows Corridor B of the Appalachian Development Highway System and serves as a major thoroughfare in the Tri-Cities. The entire route of USÂ 23 in Tennessee is an interstate-grade freeway. 2

Route description

USÂ 23, in concurrency with I-26, enters Unicoi County at Sam's Gap (el. 3,799Â ft (1,158Â m)) and travels north along Stamp Ridge. Immediately after crossing the state line is a truck information station, signed "Truck Inspection," which requires all trucks to go through before continuing. The following 3 miles (4.8Â km) is a 6% grade descent, accompanied with two runaway truck ramps, into the South Indian Creek valley. Between mile markers 47-48 is a scenic overlook turnoff (northbound USÂ 23/westbound I-26 only), followed by the I-26 Erwin/Unicoi County Welcome Center, located on Clear Branch Road exit. At Temple Hill, USÂ 19W, traveling from Burnsville, joins in concurrency. 3

After crossing the Nolichucky River, USÂ 23 travels along the eastern foot of Buffalo Mountain as it goes through the towns of Erwin and Unicoi. Entering first in Carter County, USÂ 23 enters Johnson City at the Okolona Road exit. Crossing soon afterwards into Washington County, USÂ 23 connects with USÂ 321/SRÂ 67, which goes to Elizabethton. At the Roan Street exit, USÂ 19W splits off and joins USÂ 11E, continuing towards Bluff City. Passing through Indian Ridge, USÂ 23 leaves Johnson City proper, continuing northwesterly into Sullivan County. 4

After crossing the county and also entering Kingsport city limits, USÂ 23 connects with I-81, in a cloverleaf interchange. At mile marker five is the I-26 Kingsport/Sullivan County Welcome Center, which is accessible from both directions. Staying west of downtown Kingsport, USÂ 23 cuts through Bays Mountain and then crosses the south fork of the Holston River. At the USÂ 11W (West Stone Drive) exit is the western terminus of I-26. With hidden concurrency with SRÂ 137, USÂ 23 continues north into Virginia, at Long Ridge, continuing towards Gate City. 5

State RouteÂ 137 (SRÂ 137) is a hidden concurrency that overlaps USÂ 23 between the USÂ 11W (West Stone Drive) interchange and the Virginia state line, all within Kingsport. Established in 1970 as a four-lane divided freeway, it traversed between SRÂ 354, in Johnson City and SRÂ 93, in Kingsport. SRÂ 137 then expanded in phases: In 1973, extended south to USÂ 11E/US 19W; in 1976, extended south to SRÂ 91; in 1978, extended south to USÂ 321; by 1980, extended north to USÂ 11W; and in 1985, extended north to the Virginia state line and south to Erwin. In December, 1985, I-181 was established, truncating SRÂ 137 to its current routing today. 9

History

USÂ 23 was established in 1930 with a complete concurrency with SRÂ 36, from the North Carolina state line to the Virginia state line; going through Erwin, Unicoi, Johnson City and Kingsport. In 1952, USÂ 23 was rerouted southwest of Ernestville, along SRÂ 81 through Flag Pond and Sam's Gap into North Carolina; its old alignment remained part of USÂ 19W/SRÂ 36. 10

In 1970, The first segment of a four-lane freeway, designated SRÂ 137, was opened between Johnson City and Kingsport. Over the following decade, SRÂ 137 continued being extended at both ends until in 1982, when AASHTO approved USÂ 23 relocation onto the freeway, leaving behind its old alignment to SRÂ 36 north of Johnson City to near the Virginia state line. In December 1985, I-181 was established, truncated SRÂ 137 north of USÂ 11W, while SRÂ 36 replaced as new concurrency south of USÂ 11E; all signs and exit numbers were based on USÂ 23 designation. In 1988, AASHTO approved an extension of I-26 from North Carolina to I-81, on contingent upon the completion and certification of interstate standards in North Carolina, which happened in August 2003. Following the new designation, both SRÂ 36 and SRÂ 81 was removed of their concurrencies with USÂ 23. In March 2007, the remaining spur of I-181, from I-81 to USÂ 11W, was switched to I-26. 11

InterstateÂ 181 (I-181) was established in December 1985 as an interstate designation of USÂ 23, which was already built to interstate standards in the 1970s. I-181 traversed from USÂ 321/SRÂ 67, in Johnson City, to USÂ 11W/SRÂ 1, in Kingsport, totaling 23.85 miles (38.38Â km). USÂ 23 continued on both directions as interstate grade to the Virginia line, to the north, and 15 miles (24Â km) south to Erwin; by 1992, USÂ 23 was upgraded to interstate grade south to Sam's Gap, at the North Carolina state line. All exit numbers were based on USÂ 23 mileage. On August 5, 2003, after completion of a 9-mile (14Â km) section completed in North Carolina, I-26 was extended west into Tennessee, replacing I-181 from Johnson City to I-81; north of I-81, I-181 continued into Kingsport. The American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials (AASHTO) initially ruled against an extension of I-26 (as the number) along the remainder of I-181 to Kingsport, since that would give a main route Interstate Highway (I-26) a so-called "stub end," not connecting to any other Interstate highway, to an international border, or to a seacoast. The numerical extension was in 2005 enacted by the effect of the Safe, Accountable, Flexible, Efficient Transportation Equity Act: A Legacy for Users (SAFETEA-LU), signed into law on August 10, 2005. In March 2007, I-181 was officially decommissioned, as all signs and exit numbers were changed-over to I-26's designation. 13
