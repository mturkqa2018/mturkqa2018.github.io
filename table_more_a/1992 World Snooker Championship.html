The 1992 World Snooker Championship (also referred to as the 1992 Embassy World Snooker Championship for the purposes of sponsorship) was a professional ranking snooker tournament that took place between 18 April and 4 May 1992 at the Crucible Theatre in Sheffield, England. 2

John Parrott was the defending champion but he lost 12â€“13 to Alan McManus in the quarter final and fell to the Crucible curse, becoming another champion unable to defend his first world title. 3

Stephen Hendry was the eventual winner, after defeating Jimmy White 18â€“14 in the final. At one point White led by 14â€“8, but Hendry then won 10 successive frames to secure his second World Championship title. The tournament was sponsored by cigarette manufacturer Embassy. 4

Tournament summary

  • Debutants at the Crucible this year were Peter Ebdon, Mark Johnston-Allen, Mick Price, Chris Small, Stephen Murphy, Nigel Bond and James Wattana. Ebdon, Price, Small and Wattana reached the second round.
  • Jimmy White became only the second player ever to compile a maximum break at the Crucible, during his 10â€“4 first round win over Tony Drago, nine years after Cliff Thorburn's maximum in 1983. The highest break of the qualifying stage was 141 made by Joe Johnson.
  • Hendry secured a record in the semi-final, when he beat Terry Griffiths 16â€“4. This was the biggest semi-final victory at the World Championship, until it was superseded in 2004 by Ronnie O'Sullivan, who defeated Hendry 17â€“4.
  • Steve Davis' 4â€“10 defeat to qualifier Ebdon was his first defeat in the opening round since 1982. It also ended his nine-year streak of reaching at least the semi-finals of the tournament.
  • The 10â€“0 win by defending champion John Parrott over Eddie Charlton was the first ever whitewash in the history of the Crucible. There would not be another whitewash until Shaun Murphy defeated Luo Honghao in 2019.
  • Eight of the sixteen seeded players exited the tournament in the first round. This did not happen again until 2012.
