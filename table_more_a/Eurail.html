The Eurail Pass, launched in 1959 and known formerly known as 'Europass' or 'Eurorail Pass', is a train pass that allows customers to travel around 31 European countries on nearly all European railroads and some shipping lines. The Eurail Group, based in Utrecht, the Netherlands, is responsible for the marketing and management of the Eurail and Interrail Passes. The company is owned by more than 35 indiviual European railway and shipping companies. The Eurail Pass is available to non-European citizens, whereas the Interrail Pass introduced later in 1972, is available for European residents. These passes provide access to 250,000 km of European railway and are utilised by over 33 thousand travellers every year. 1

Eligibility

The Eurail pass is available to non-European citizens. 2

The Interrail Pass, which is available to citizens and official residents of countries in the European Union, as well as the following countries outside the European Union: Albania, Andorra, Belarus, Bosnia-Herzegovina, North Macedonia, Gibraltar, Iceland, Kosovo, Liechtenstein, Moldova, Monaco, Montenegro, Norway, Russian Federation, San Marino, Serbia, Switzerland, Turkey, Ukraine, and Vatican City. 3

To obtain an Interrail pass, proof of citizenship must be established with a passport or identity card, or proof of residency must be established with government issued residency documents. 4

Traveler Categories

There are 4 categories of Eurail Passes available: 5

  • Children aged 4 to 11 years old can travel free of charge when accompanied by a full-paying adult (maximum of two children per adult pass).
  • Child Pass: For travellers who are younger than 12 years old.
  • Youth Pass: For travellers who are 12 to 27 years old.
  • Adult Pass: For travellers who are 28 years old or over.
  • Senior Pass: For travellers who are over 60 years old.

Pass Types

Eurail Global Pass

Countries in which the Eurail Global Pass is valid

Pass Coverage

The Eurail Global Pass is valid in all 31 participating countries. As of 2019 these include: Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Montenegro, the Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, and the United Kingdom. 6

Pass Validity

The price of the Eurail Global Pass varies depending on its validity. The validates available include: 7

  • 3 days within 1 month
  • 5 days within 1 month
  • 7 days within 1 month
  • 10 days within 2 months
  • 15 days within 2 months
  • 15 days
  • 22 days
  • 1 month
  • 2 months
  • 3 months

Eurail One Country Pass

Pass Coverage

The Eurail One Country Pass allows customers to travel by train across the national rail network of only one country, with unlimited trains rides permitted on each travel day. These passes are available within the following countries: Austria, Benelux, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Greece, Greek Islands, Hungary, Ireland (Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland), Italy, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Scandinavia, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and Turkey. 8

There are two exceptions where travel in multiple countries is permitted using a Eurail One Country Pass. The One Country Pass Benelux permits travel in Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg region, and the One Country Pass Scandinavia permits travel in Denmark, Norway, Sweden, and Finland. 9

Currently, Eurail One Country Passes are not available in all European countries. Countries without these passes include Bosnia, Germany, Montenegro, and Switzerland. 10

Pass Validity

The Eurail One Country Passes are available for travel on 3, 4, 5, 6 or 8 travel days within a 1 month period. 11

Seat Reservations on High Speed, Overnight, and other Trains

2

Seats on Thalys Train

Reservations for Eurail Pass holders are not usually required for local or regional trains, however a reservation is needed for most high-speed, international, and overnight trains. Additional fee surcharges are often required to guarantee secured seat reservations, as well as supplementary add-on benefits such as meals, drinks, free Wi-Fi etc. These fees vary between different carriers, agents and routes, but travellers can usually avoid paying excess fees by taking regional or local trains instead. Reservations can be made at the station, online on the relevant carrier site, on the Eurail site, in the Rail Planner App, by phone through call centres, or at a travel agency. 12

High speed trains

Many high speed trains require reservation and additional payments of extra fees. Examples include: 13

  • Eurostar (London, Paris, Amsterdam, and Brussels): 2nd Class = €10 to €35, 1st Class = €15 to €43.
  • Thalys (Paris to Brussels, Amsterdam, and Cologne): 2nd Class = €15 to €25, 1st Class = €25 to €30.
  • TGV (Domestic routes): 1st or 2nd Class from €10 to €20.
  • Trenitalia (Italy): Frecciabianca, Frecciargento, Frecciarossa 1st or 2nd Class €10.
  • AVE (Spain): 2nd Class €10, 1st Class €13 or €23.50. Reservation fees for 2nd class (€6.50) and 1st Class (€10) are also required on most other long distance trains in Spain (e.g. Arco, Euromed, Alvia, Alaris, Altaria).
  • In Germany and Austria, virtually all of the fast InterCity and InterCityExpress trains can be boarded without queueing for additional tickets or supplements of any kind. ICE (Germany) trains do not require reservations, but it is voluntarily available at €4,50 for 2nd Class and €5,90 for 1st Class.
  • SJ high speed train (in Sweden, reaching Copenhagen): €7 (2nd class), €17 (1st class).
  • Some scenic trains have the option to travel in a special panoramic coach, which requires a reservation.

Further information on seat reservation fees and supplements on daytime high speed trains can be obtained from the official Eurail site. 14

Overnight trains

3

Economy Single compartment on an ÖBB double-decker sleeping car

Many overnight trains in Europe require reservations with varying additional costs depending on the customer's preferred sleeping accommodation, including options such as couchettes or sleeping cabins. For Flexi Global Pass holders, overnight trains boarded before midnight are considered as only one day of travel (recorded as the day of departure) if the customer does not change trains after midnight. Although, the pass must be valid for both the arrival and departure dates for this rule to apply. 15

Reductions on privately owned trains

Eurail and Interrail Passes are usually only valid on the national railway system of the participating countries. Many countries also have separate private railway systems, with some offering free or reduced tickets to Eurail or Interrail Pass holder. Normally, the reductions are between 25% and 50%. 16

History

  • 1959: Eurail is founded and valid in 13 countries.
  • 1971: Student Railpass (2nd class) was introduced.
  • 1980: Eurail become valid in 16 countries.
  • 1991: Eurail Pass expands to the former East Germany, allowing travel throughout reunified Germany.
  • 2001: Launch of Eurail Select Pass.
  • 2007: Eurail Pass is renamed Eurail Global Pass.
  • 2008: Slovenian and Croatian railways join and the Pass becomes valid in 20 countries.
  • 2009: 50th Anniversary Eurail Pass. Eurail is valid in 21 countries.
  • 2013: Launch of the Rail Planner App.
  • 2015: 2 children aged 11 and under can travel for free with at least 1 adult. Poland, Montenegro, Bosnia-Herzegovina, and Serbia join Eurail Global Pass.
  • 2016: Eurail Global Pass covers 28 countries. All Passes become available to purchase 11 months in advance.
  • 2017: Eurostar joins Eurail Group. Youth Pass age increases to 27. Introduction of 2 new Pass options: Eurail One Country France Pass and Eurail Italy Switzerland Select Pass.
  • 2019: The pass portfolio now includes Global Pass and One Country Pass only (removal of Select Pass). The Saver Pass type is removed. 1st and 2nd class are available for all Products (except Greek Islands domestic). Furthermore, Britain, FYR Macedonia and Lithuania join Eurail. The New Greek Islands Pass is now also valid for 5 domestic trips and increases to 53 islands.
