The Kingdom of Powo was a kingdom located in present-day BomÃª County, Tibet. The ruler of Powo used the title Kanam Gyelpo (kaH gnam rgyal po) or Kanam Depa (kaH gnam sde pa). 1

It was said that the monarchs of Powo were descendants of Drigum Tsenpo. The kingdom was annexed by Qing China in 1910, but the King restored in the next year. In 1928, the kingdom was annexed by Tibet. 2
