The Bangladeshi passport is a passport issued to the citizens of Bangladesh for the purpose of international travel. The passport is issued by the Government of The People's Republic of Bangladesh or by any of its overseas missions to Bangladeshi citizens who are citizens by birth or by descent. 2

The passport of Bangladesh is valid in all countries of the world for travel except Israel, with which there are no diplomatic relations established. Formerly, Bangladeshi passports were also not valid to travel to Taiwan, Rhodesia and South Africa. 3

History

Prior to the machine-readable passport, Bangladesh was one of the few last remaining countries to still issue handwritten passports. In 2010, the Bangladesh government announced to replace over 6.6Â million handwritten passports with the new machine-readable passports. According to a guideline of the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), machine-readable passport (MRP) and machine-readable visa (MRV) issuance was started by the Bangladesh government in April 2010. However, all traditional handwritten passports were honoured as bona fide travel documents issued by the Bangladesh government until they expired. All traditional handwritten passports were successfully withdrawn from circulation before ICAO's international deadline of November 2015. 4

As Bangladesh moved from handwritten passports to machine-readable passports, the Bangladeshi government in 2016 announced its plans to soon introduce the issuance of biometric passports for all its citizens, which would ease immigration and increase visa-free access worldwide for Bangladeshi passport holders. 5

Languages

The text of Bangladeshi passports is printed in both Bangla (Bengali) and English. 10

Passport holder identity

Information about the passport holder are spread across seven or more pages in the contemporary Bangladesh passport. The field names (e.g. name) are written in Bengali (Bangla) and English, with the field values is written in English only. No information regarding gender or faith are required. 11

The inside cover of a machine-readable Bangladeshi passport has an embossed image of the National Monument, with the national anthem of Bangladesh written in Bengali (Bangla) and English. 12

The first page contains the President's declaration in Bengali and English, and states that the passport is valid for all the countries in the world except Israel. 13
